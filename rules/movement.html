Movement

Rule Dependency: Action Points

This set of rules is designed to work with the Action Points system.
When you move, you travel a distance using any movement modes of one of your speeds. When you can move and no speed is specified, you choose which speed(s) to use. When using multiple speeds in the same move, keep track of how much movement you spend; you can't keep moving with a speed once you've spent more movement than the speed allows. For example, if you have a walk speed of 30 feet and a fly speed of 60 feet, you can fly 20 feet and then walk 10 feet. At this point, you can't walk anymore, but you can still fly up to 30 more feet.

Related Actions

The Move action is a non-repeatable, non-atomic free action that lets you move up to your speed. The Dash action is a repeatable, non-atomic standard action that also lets you move up to your speedThese actions are kept separate because some abilities interact with the Dash action, but not the Move action..

Forced Movement

Movement caused by a push, pull, or other external force is called forced movement. An effect that moves you specifies how far you are moved, ignoring difficult terrain and similar hindrances. Note that an effect with the phrase "when you move" isn't triggered by forced movement. For example, if grappling lets you drag a creature along with you when you move, you don't drag the creature if you are pushed.

Movement Modes

A movement mode lets you move in a particular way. You can use a movement mode actively or passively if you have a speed that gives access to it. For example, if you have a walk speed, you can use the walking movement mode actively to walk across the ground, and you use it passively to avoid falling prone.You can benefit from the passive use of a movement mode as long as you have a speed giving access to that mode, even if that speed is 0.

A movement modifier is a special movement mode that affects how you move.

Speeds

A speed has an associated distance and grants access to one or more movement modes. Moving with a speed's natural movement mode has a base cost of 1 foot per foot traveled; non-natural modes cost an additional foot per foot traveled. Some speeds have unique benefits or drawbacks to using them.
An effect that changes "your speed" changes each of your speeds.
