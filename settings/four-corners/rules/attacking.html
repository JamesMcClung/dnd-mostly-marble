Attacking

An attack is either melee or ranged, and either weapon or spell.

Unarmed Attacks

Every creature has access to the following unarmed attacks, which are melee weapon attacks. By default, an unarmed attack has a reach of 5 feet, and you are not proficient with it.
If an unarmed attack causes a saving throw, the DC equals your passive Athletics score.
Unarmed Strike. You punch, kick, or otherwise strike at an opponent with your body. On a hit, you deal 1d1 bludgeoning, piercing, or slashing damage as appropriate. Unarmed strikes count as simple light melee weapons.
Grapple. You attempt to grab an opponent with a free hand. On a hit, the target must succeed on a Strength saving throw or be grappled by you, with you as the grappling creature.
Push. You attempt to push an opponent. On a hit, the target must succeed on a Strength saving throw or be pushed up to 5 feet away from you.
Pull. You attempt to pull an opponent with a free hand. On a hit, the target must succeed on a Strength saving throw or be pulled up to 5 feet closer to you.
Trip. You attempt to knock an opponent prone. On a hit, the target must succeed on a Strength saving throw or fall prone.

Grappling

While you are grappling another creature (the grappled creature), you experience the following effects:
  • Your speed is halved if you are the same size as the grappled creature, or 0 if you are smaller.
  • You have disadvantage on attack rolls against a target other than the grappled creature.
  • When you move on your turn, you drag the grappled creature with you.
You can end the grapple at any time, no action required. The grapple also ends if you become incapacitated or if the grappled creature is no longer in your reach.

Grappled

While you are grappled by another creature (the grappling creature), you experience the following effects:
  • Your speed is halved if you are one size larger than the grappling creature, or 0 if you are the same size or smaller.
  • You have disadvantage on attack rolls against a target other than the grappling creature.
  • When you move on your turn, you drag the grappling creature with you.
As an action, you can repeat the saving throw against the grapple, ending the grapple on a success.
