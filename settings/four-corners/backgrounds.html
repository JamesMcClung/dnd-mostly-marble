Backgrounds

Here is a list of all backgrounds.

Dwarven Combat Training

You were trained in a Dwarven style of combat, which emphasizes confidence and ferocity. You can speak, read, and write Dwarvish. You are also proficient with the battleaxe, handaxe, greataxe, light hammer, warhammer, maul, war pick, medium and heavy armor, and shields. While you are wielding one of these weapons, you have advantage on Intimidation checks. While you are wearing such armor, you have advantage on saving throws against being frightened.

Dwarven Trade Training

You are experienced in a Dwarven trade. You can speak, read, and write Dwarvish, and pick one of the following benefits:

  1. You are proficient with brewer's supplies, and have expertise in History for the purpose of Intelligence checks relating to the identification of ale.
  2. You are proficient with jeweler's tools, and have expertise in History for the purpose of Intelligence checks relating to the origin of cut and uncut gems.
  3. You are proficient with mason's tools, and have expertise in History for the purpose of Intelligence checks relating to the origin of stonework.
  4. You are proficient with smith's tools, and have expertise in History for the purpose of Intelligence checks relating to the origin of forged arms and armor.

Long Past

Prerequisite: At least 200 years old

You have done much in your long life. You may have spent time in multiple professions, or perhaps you dedicated your life to mastery of a single skill. Choose one of the following options:

  • You gain two additional backgrounds of your choice.
  • You gain one additional background of your choice. If that background grants proficiency in a weapon, you gain a +1 bonus to attack rolls with that weapon. If it grants any skill or tool proficiencies, you gain expertise instead. If it does not grant any skill proficiencies, you gain expertise in one tool or skill of your choice that you are already proficient in.

High-Elven Apprenticeship

You apprenticed under a High Elf wizard. You are proficient with Arcana and a one-handed melee weapon of your choice; you can speak, read, and write Elvish and one other language of your choice; and you learn prestidigitation and blade ward. After you cast blade ward, the first creature you deal damage to with a melee weapon or evocation spell before the end of your next turn takes an extra 1d8 force damage.

Wood-Elven Apprenticeship

You apprenticed under a Wood Elf druid or ranger. You are proficient with Nature, shortbows, and longbows; you can speak, read, and write Elvish; and you learn druidcraft and shillelagh. You can cast shillelagh on a bow, allowing you to use Wisdom instead of Dexterity for the weapon's attack and damage rolls.

Wood-Elven Combat Training

You were trained in the combat style of the Wood Elves, who hide in trees and retreat when outmatched. You can speak, read, and write Elvish. You are also proficient with the shortsword, rapier, shortbow, and longbow. While you are wielding one of these melee weapons, opportunity attacks against you have disadvantage. You can also attack silently with these ranged weapons so as not to reveal your location while Hidden. Instead, you just reroll the Hide DC.

Sea-Elven Combat Training

You were trained in the style of combat of the Sea Elves, or Maredrin, who use underwater guerrilla tactics. You can speak, read, and write Marese. You are also proficient with the dagger, shortsword, and light armor. Your melee attacks with these weapons ignore half cover. While underwater and wearing light or no armor, half cover grants you a +3 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws instead of +2.

Maledrin Combat Training

You were trained in the combat style of the Jungle Elves, or Maladrin, who specialize in taking captives. You can speak, read, and write Malese. You are also proficient with the club, spear, net, and blowgun. For you, the net has a range of 10/30 feet, and you can apply poison to a blowgun dart as part of the attack used to fire it.

Maledrin Acolyte

Prerequisite: Maledrin

You were a Maledrin acolyte being trained in the secrets of priesthood. You can speak Deep Speech, which you use to conduct rituals and commune with your lesser gods. You learn each of the following spells once you have a spell slot capable of casting it: arms of Hadar, augury, hunger of Hadar, Evard's black tentacles, and contact other plane. You also know the primal savagery cantrip. When you cast it using this background, your arms turn into tentacles and you have a reach of 10 feet. These spells are cast using Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma, chosen when you take this background.

Gnomish Trade Training

You know Gnomish secrets of alchemy or artifice. You can speak, read, and write a Gabrorian dialect of Gnomish, and pick one of the following benefits:

  1. You are proficient with tinker's tools, and have expertise in History for the purpose of Intelligence checks related to magical or technological devices.
  2. You are proficient with alchemist's tools, and have expertise in History for the purpose of Intelligence checks related to magical or chemical substances.
