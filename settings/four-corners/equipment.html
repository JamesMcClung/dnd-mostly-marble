Improvised Weapons

Sometimes characters don't have their weapons and have to attack with whatever is close at hand. An improvised weapon includes any object you can wield in one or two hands, such as broken glass, a table leg, a frying pan, a wagon wheel, or a dead goblin.
In many cases, an improvised weapon is similar to an actual weapon and can be treated as such. For example, a table leg is akin to a club. At the DM's option, a character proficient with a weapon can use a similar object as if it were that weapon and use his or her proficiency bonus.
An object that bears no resemblance to a weapon deals 1d4 damage (the DM assigns a damage type appropriate to the object). If a character uses a ranged weapon to make a melee attack, or throws a melee weapon that does not have the thrown property, it also deals 1d4 damage. An improvised thrown weapon has a normal range of 20 feet and a long range of 60 feet.

Silvered Weapons

Some monsters that have immunity or resistance to normal weapons are susceptible to silver weapons, so cautious adventurers invest extra coin to plate their weapons with silver. You can silver a single weapon or ten pieces of ammunition for 100 gp. This cost represents not only the price of the silver, but the time and expertise needed to add silver to the weapon without making it less effective.

Unarmed Attacks

All characters can make attacks with their body. Unarmed attacks count as simple melee weapons. Different unarmed attacks can be used to damage, push, pull, trip, or grapple an opponent.

Unarmed Strike

Melee weapon attack
You punch, kick, or otherwise strike at an opponent with your body. On a hit, you deal 1d1 bludgeoning, piercing, or slashing damage as appropriate.
Unarmed strikes count as simple melee weapons with the light property.

Grapple

Melee weapon attack
You attempt to grab an opponent with a free hand. On a hit, the target is grappled by you, with you as the grappling creature. The escape DC is equal to your passive Athletics score.
Grapples count as simple melee weapons.

Push

Melee weapon attack
You attempt to push an opponent. On a hit, the target must succeed on a Strength saving throw against your passive Athletics score or be pushed up to 5 feet away from you.
Pushes count as simple melee weapons.

Pull

Melee weapon attack
You attempt to pull an opponent with a free hand. On a hit, the target must succeed on a Strength saving throw against your passive Athletics score or be pulled up to 5 feet closer to you.
Pulls count as simple melee weapons.

Trip

Melee weapon attack
You attempt to knock an opponent prone. On a hit, the target must succeed on a Strength saving throw against your passive Athletics score or fall prone.
Trips count as simple melee weapons.

Armor Proficiency. If you wear armor that you lack proficiency with, you have disadvantage on any ability check, saving throw, or attack roll that involves Strength or Dexterity, and you can't cast spells.
Shield Proficiency. You can only benefit from the AC bonus of a shield if you have proficiency with shields and with the shield's category of armor (light, medium, or heavy). For example, if you have proficiency with light armor, medium armor, and shields, you can use a buckler, round shield, or kite shield, but not a pavise.
Armor Class (AC). Armor protects its wearer from attacks. The armor (and shield) you wear determines your base Armor Class.
Shields. A shield is made from wood or metal and is carried in one hand. Wielding a shield increases your Armor Class by the specified amount. You can benefit from only one shield at a time.

Getting Into and Out of Armor

The time it takes to don or doff armor depends on the armor's category.
Don. This is the time it takes to put on armor. You benefit from the armor's AC only if you take the full time to don the suit of armor.
Doff. This is the time it takes to take off armor. If you have help, reduce this time by half.

Thrown

If a weapon has the thrown property, you can throw the weapon to make a ranged attack. If the weapon is a melee weapon, you use the same ability modifier for that attack roll and damage roll that you would use for a melee attack with the weapon. For example, if you throw a handaxe, you use your Strength, but if you throw a dagger, you can use either your Strength or your Dexterity, since the dagger has the finesse property.

Light

Light

A light weapon is small and easy to handle, making it ideal for use when fighting with two weapons. When you take the Attack action and attack with a light melee weapon that you're holding in one hand, you can use a bonus action to attack with a different light melee weapon that you're holding in the other hand. You don't add your ability modifier to the damage of the bonus attack, unless that modifier is negative.
If either weapon has the thrown property, you can throw the weapon, instead of making a melee attack with it.

range

Range

A weapon that can be used to make a ranged attack has a range shown in parentheses after the ammunition or thrown property. The range lists two numbers. The first is the weapon's normal range in feet, and the second indicates the weapon's maximum range. When attacking a target beyond normal range, you have disadvantage on the attack roll. You can't attack a target beyond the weapon's long range.

Ammunition

Ammunition

You can use a weapon that has the ammunition property to make a ranged attack only if you have ammunition to fire from the weapon. Each time you attack with the weapon, you expend one piece of ammunition. Drawing the ammunition from a quiver, case, or other container is part of the attack. At the end of the battle, you can recover half your expended ammunition by taking a minute to search the battlefield.
If you use a weapon that has the ammunition property to make a melee attack, you treat the weapon as an improvised weapon. A sling must be loaded to deal any damage when used in this way.

Special

A Large or smaller creature hit by a net is restrained until it is freed. A net has no effect on creatures that are formless, or creatures that are Huge or larger. A creature can use its action to make a DC 10 Strength check, freeing itself or another creature within its reach on a success. When you use an action, bonus action, or reaction to attack with a net, you can make only one attack regardless of the number of attacks you can normally make.

Attack

When you take the Attack action, you make one weapon attack. See the special attacks section for additional options.
You can equip or unequip one weapon before or after each attack you make as part of this action, even if the attack is unarmed.
If you move on your turn, you can use some or all of that movement to move between the attacks of this action if you have a feature, such as Extra Attack, that gives you more than one attack as part of the Attack action.

Grappling

While you are grappling another creature (the grappled creature), you experience the following effects:
  • Your speed is halved if you are the same size as the grappled creature, or 0 if you are smaller.
  • You have disadvantage on attack rolls against a target other than the grappled creature.
  • When you move on your turn, you drag the grappled creature with you.
You can end the grapple at any time, no action required. The grapple also ends if you become incapacitated or if the grappled creature is no longer within your reach.
This condition is commonly acquired via the grapple special attack.

grappled

Grappled

While you are grappled by another creature (the grappling creature), you experience the following effects:
  • Your speed is halved if you are one size larger than the grappling creature, or 0 if you are the same size or smaller.
  • You have disadvantage on attack rolls against a target other than the grappling creature.
  • When you move on your turn, you drag the grappling creature with you.
As an action, you can make a saving throw against the grapple, ending the grapple on a success.
This condition can be commonly applied via the grapple special attack.

prone

Prone

While prone, you experience the following effects:
  • Your only movement option is to crawl, unless you stand up and thereby end the condition.
  • You have disadvantage on attack rolls.
  • An attack roll against you has advantage if the attacker is within 5 feet of you. Otherwise, the attack roll has disadvantage.
Standing up costs an amount of movement equal to half your current speed. You can't stand up if you don't have enough movement left or if your speed is 0.
This condition can be commonly applied via the Trip special attack.
