Genasi

Genasi are individuals infused with an element: air, earth, fire, or water. A genasi can be born to parents of any race.

Fall of Nature

Legends tell of genasi who could rival a Primordial with their elemental power. Such legends may have been exaggerated, but genasi power is certainly in decline. In living memory, no genasi has been more innately powerful than a typical elemental. It is postulated that the binding of the Primordials is somehow the cause of the decline.

Game Mechanics

Genasi

Abilities. Genasi inherit the abilities of their parents, with some alterations. Earth and water genasi tend to be stronger and slower, while air and fire genasi tend to be weaker and faster. All varieties of genasi have a higher-than-average Constitution.

Age. Genasi are generally healthier than normal, but have the same lifespan as their parents.

Alignment. Genasi have no supernatural proclivities towards any alignment. A genasi's upbringing may be unusual and influence their alignment, but genasi generally do not have such cursed childhoods as tieflings or exalted childhoods as aasimar.

Inherited Traits. Your size, base walking speed, and languages are inherited from your parents' race. You also inherit any trait that grants a climbing, swimming, or flying speed, or the ability to breathe underwater.

Elemental Soul. Your creature type is Humanoid, but you also register as an Elemental to abilities that detect elementals.

Air Genasi

Air Resistances. You have resistance to thunder damage and to bludgeoning damage inflicted by wind.

Air Spells. You know the gust cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you learn the catapult spell. When you reach 5th level, you learn the warding wind spell. You can cast each of these spells once with this trait and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells when you cast them with this trait (choose when you select this race), and you require no material components when you do so.

Aerial Grace. As a bonus action, you can focus on the air around you to support your weight and guide your movement. While you are in air and maintain concentration, as if on a spell, you gain the following benefits:

  • You and anything you wear weighs half as much as normal, and your jump distance is doubled.
  • When you fall, your rate of descent cannot exceed 60 feet per round. You take no falling damage and can land on your feet, and you can travel 1 foot horizontally for every 1 foot you descend by spending 1 foot of movement.
  • You can hedge out fog, smoke, and other gases from your immediate vicinity, so you only breathe or touch clean air.
  • You have advantage on Acrobatics checks.

Earth Genasi

Earth Resistances. You have resistance to lightning damage and to bludgeoning damage inflicted by earth.

Earth Spells. You know the move earth cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you learn the earth tremor spell. When you reach 5th level, you learn the earthen grasp spell. You can cast each of these spells once with this trait and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells when you cast them with this trait (choose when you select this race), and you require no material components when you do so.

Earthen Strength. As a bonus action, you can focus on the earth beneath you to grant you strength and stability. While you are on the ground and maintain concentration, as if on a spell, you gain the following benefits:

  • You cannot be pushed or pulled against your will, and you have advantage on saving throws against being knocked prone.
  • You ignore difficult terrain made of earth or stone.
  • Any nonmagical bludgeoning, piercing, or slashing damage you take from a weapon attack is reduced by 1. This reduction is applied before any resistances and vulnerabilities.
  • You have advantage on Athletics checks.

Fire Genasi

Fire Resistance. You have resistance to fire damage.

Fire Spells. You know the control flames cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you learn the burning hands spell. When you reach 5th level, you learn the flaming sphere spell. You can cast each of these spells once with this trait and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells when you cast them with this trait (choose when you select this race), and you require no material components when you do so.

Fiery Brilliance. As a bonus action, you can let fire consume and invigorate you. While you maintain concentration, as if on a spell, you gain the following benefits:

  • Harmless wisps of fire coil off you, shedding bright light out to a radius of 5 feet and dim light an additional 5 feet.
  • When you would take fire damage, you can use your reaction to become immune to the triggering fire damage.
  • You gain a ranged spell attack. The attack has a range of 30 feet. You are proficient with it, and you use the ability you chose for your Fire Spells trait for its attack and damage rolls. Its damage is fire, and its damage die is a d6.
  • Once on each of your turns when you cause a creature to take fire damage, you can cause that creature to take additional fire damage equal to your proficiency bonus.
  • You have advantage on Intimidation checks.

Water Genasi

Water Resistance. You have resistance to acid damage and to bludgeoning damage inflicted by water.

Water Spells. You know the shape water cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you learn the fog cloud spell. When you reach 5th level, you learn the binding ice spell. You can cast each of these spells once with this trait and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells when you cast them with this trait (choose when you select this race), and you require no material components when you do so.

Aquatic Serenity. As a bonus action, you can attune yourself with the water within and around you. While you maintain concentration, as if on a spell, you gain the following benefits:

  • You have a swimming speed of 60 feet.
  • You can maintain a bubble around your head that either contains or excludes water, potentially allowing you to breathe.
  • You can use your action to touch a creature and allow it to spend 1 hit die, roll it, and regain hit points equal to the result (its Constitution modifier is not added). This action has no effect on undead or constructs.
  • Once on each of your turns when you cause a creature to regain hit points, that creature can regain additional hit points equal to your proficiency bonus.
  • You have advantage on Medicine checks.
