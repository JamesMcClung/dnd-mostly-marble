Genasi

Genasi

Genasi are individuals infused with an element: air, earth, fire, or water. A genasi can be born to parents of any race.

Fall of Nature

Legends tell of genasi who could rival a Primordial with their elemental power. Such legends may have been exaggerated, but genasi power is certainly in decline. In living memory, no genasi has been more innately powerful than a typical elemental. It is postulated that the binding of the Primordials is somehow the cause of the decline.

Game Mechanics

Genasi

Abilities. Genasi have the following ability score tendencies.
Inherited Attributes. Genasi generally inherit the abilities of their parents, in addition to the supernatural enhancements associated with being a genasi.
Supernatural Fortitude. All varieties of genasi have a predisposition towards a high Constitution score.
Vast Strength. Earth and water genasi tend to be heavier than their size would suggest and have a high average Strength score.
Fleeting Agility. Air and fire genasi are unusually light and thus agile, often leading to a high Dexterity score.
Alignment. Genasi have no supernatural proclivities towards any alignment. A genasi's upbringing may be unusual and influence their alignment, but genasi generally do not have such cursed childhoods as tieflings or exalted childhoods as aasimar.
Age. Genasi are generally healthier than normal, but have the same old age threshold and aging rate as their parents.
Inherited Traits. Your size, base walking speed, and languages are inherited from your parents' race, which can be any race of your choice that doesn't also have this trait. You also inherit any trait that grants a climb, swim, or fly speed, or the ability to breathe underwater.
Elemental Soul. Your creature type is Humanoid, but you also register as an Elemental to abilities that detect elementals.

Air Genasi

Air Resistances. You have resistance to thunder damage and to bludgeoning damage inflicted by wind.
Soul of Air. You know the gust cantrip. You can learn additional spells from this trait if you take the Air Genasi Magic feat. You can cast each of these spells once with this trait and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells when you cast them with this trait (choose when you select this race), and you require no material components when you do so.
Jet Boost. As a minor action, you can propel yourself in a straight line in any direction a number of feet equal to 5 times your proficiency bonus. This motion does not provoke opportunity attacks.
Unending Breath. You can hold your breath indefinitely while you're not incapacitated.
Aerial Grace. As a minor action, you can begin to focus on the air around and use it to guide your movement. While you are in air, unencumbered, and maintain concentration, you gain the following benefits:
  • You weigh half as much as normal.
  • When you fall, your rate of descent cannot exceed 60 feet per round. You take no falling damage and can land on your feet, and you can travel 2 feet horizontally for every 1 foot you descend by spending 1 foot of movement.
  • You have advantage on Acrobatics checks.

Earth Genasi

Earth Resistances. You have resistance to lightning damage and to bludgeoning damage inflicted by earth.
Soul of Earth. You know the mold earth cantrip. You can learn additional spells from this trait if you take the Earth Genasi Magic feat. You can cast each of these spells once with this trait and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells when you cast them with this trait (choose when you select this race), and you require no material components when you do so.
Stonewalker. You ignore difficult terrain made of earth or stone.
Defensive Pillars. When you are on the ground and you would be hit by a ranged attack or fail a Dexterity saving throw from a source you can see, you can use your reaction to gain half cover against the triggering effect, potentially causing the attack to miss or the saving throw to succeed. Doing so takes a great amount of sudden focus; if you are concentrating when you use this reaction, you lose concentration.
Earthen Strength. As a minor action, you can begin to focus on the earth beneath you and tap into its strength and stability. While you are on the ground and maintain concentration, you gain the following benefits:
  • At the start of each of your turns, you gain temporary hit points equal to your proficiency bonus. They vanish if you lose concentration.
  • You have tremorsense out to a distance of 15 feet.
  • You have advantage on Athletics checks.

Fire Genasi

Fire Resistance. You have resistance to fire damage.
Soul of Fire. You know the control flames cantrip. You can learn additional spells from this trait if you take the Fire Genasi Magic feat. You can cast each of these spells once with this trait and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells when you cast them with this trait (choose when you select this race), and you require no material components when you do so.
Flameburst. You have a ranged spell attack. The attack has a range of 30 feet. You are proficient with it, and you use the ability you chose for your Soul of Fire trait for its attack and damage rolls. Its damage is fire, and its damage die is a d6.
Disperse Heat. When you would take fire damage, you can use your reaction to become immune to the triggering fire damage. Doing so takes a great amount of sudden focus; if you are concentrating when you use this reaction, you lose concentration.
Fiery Brilliance. As a minor action, you can let fire consume and invigorate you. While you maintain concentration, you gain the following benefits:
  • Harmless wisps of fire coil off you, shedding bright light out to a radius of 10 feet and dim light an additional 10 feet.
  • Once on each of your turns when you cause a creature to take fire damage, you can cause that creature to take additional fire damage equal to your proficiency bonus.
  • You have advantage on Intimidation checks.

Water Genasi

Water Resistance. You have resistance to acid damage and to bludgeoning damage inflicted by water.
Soul of Water. You know the shape water cantrip. You can learn additional spells from this trait if you take the Water Genasi Magic feat. You can cast each of these spells once with this trait and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells when you cast them with this trait (choose when you select this race), and you require no material components when you do so.
Waterborn. You can breathe water, and you have a swim speed of 30 feet.
Natural Healer. As a standard action, you can touch a creature and allow it to spend 1 hit die, roll it, and regain hit points equal to the result (its Constitution modifier is not added). This action has no effect on undead or constructs.
Aquatic Serenity. As a minor action, you can begin attuning yourself to the water within and around you. While you maintain concentration, you gain the following benefits:
  • Your swim speed increases to 60 feet.
  • Once on each of your turns when you cause a creature to regain hit points, that creature can regain additional hit points equal to your proficiency bonus.
  • You have advantage on Medicine checks.

Difficult Terrain

Every foot of movement in difficult terrain costs 1 extra foot. This rule is true even if multiple things in a space count as difficult terrain.
Low furniture, rubble, undergrowth, steep stairs, snow, and shallow bogs are examples of difficult terrain. The space of another creature, whether hostile or not, also counts as difficult terrain.

concentration

Concentration

Some spells and other effects require you to maintain concentration in order to keep them active. If you lose concentration, such an effect ends. You can also choose to end concentration on any effect at any time (no action required).
If a spell must be maintained with concentration, that fact appears in its Duration entry, and the spell specifies how long you can concentrate on it.
Normal activity, such as moving and attacking, doesn't interfere with concentration. The following factors can break concentration:
  • You lose concentration if you invoke another effect that requires concentration. You can't concentrate on two effects at once.
  • Whenever you take damage while you are concentrating, you must make a Constitution saving throw to maintain your concentration. The DC equals 10 or half the damage you take, whichever number is higher. If you take damage from multiple sources, such as an arrow and a dragon's breath, you make a separate saving throw for each source of damage.
  • You lose concentration on an effect if you are incapacitated or if you die.
  • Certain environmental phenomena, such as a wave crashing over you while you're on a storm-tossed ship, may require you to succeed on a DC 10 Constitution saving throw to maintain concentration. This is at your DM's discretion.

jumping

Jumping

Jumping is a movement modifier that lets you clear gaps or obstacles. Instead of the usual movement cost, you must spend 1 foot of movement for every foot you jump vertically or horizontally (whichever is higher).
When you jump, perform the following series of calculations; don't round until the end. The resulting value is the maximum number of feet you can jump.
  1. Start with your passive Athletics. Alternatively, you can make a Strength (Athletics check) and use the result instead.
  2. Divide the value by 2.
  3. If you did not get a running start immediately before jumping, divide the value by 2 again. To get a running start, you must move in a straight line in the direction you want to jump for a number of feet determined by your size, as shown in the Running Start Distances table. The speed you get a running start with must be the same as the speed you use to jump, and it must be with a natural movement mode.
  4. To determine the vertical distance you can clear, divide the value by 2 again.
If you land in difficult terrain, you must succeed on a DC 10 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check or fall prone.

cover

Cover

Walls, trees, creatures, and other obstacles can provide cover during combat, making a target more difficult to harm. A target can benefit from cover only when an attack or other effect originates on the opposite side of the cover.
There are three degrees of cover. If a target is behind multiple sources of cover, only the most protective degree of cover applies; the degrees aren't added together. For example, if a target is behind a creature that gives half cover and a tree trunk that gives three-quarters cover, the target has three-quarters cover.

Half Cover

A target with half cover has a +2 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws. A target has half cover if an obstacle blocks at least half of its body. The obstacle might be a low wall, a large piece of furniture, a narrow tree trunk, or a creature, whether that creature is an enemy or a friend.

Three-Quarters Cover

A target with three-quarters cover has a +5 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws. A target has three-quarters cover if about three-quarters of it is covered by an obstacle. The obstacle might be a portcullis, an arrow slit, or a thick tree trunk.

Total Cover

A target with total cover can't be targeted directly by an attack or a spell, although some spells can reach such a target by including it in an area of effect. A target has total cover if it is completely concealed by an obstacle.

light

Light

The presence or absence of light in an environment creates three categories of illumination: bright light, dim light, and darkness.

Bright Light

Bright light lets most creatures see normally. Even gloomy days provide bright light, as do torches, lanterns, fires, and other sources of illumination within a specific radius.

Dim Light

Dim light, also called shadows, creates a lightly obscured area. An area of dim light is usually a boundary between a source of bright light, such as a torch, and surrounding darkness. The soft light of twilight and dawn also counts as dim light. A particularly brilliant full moon might bathe the land in dim light.

Darkness

Darkness creates a heavily obscured area. Characters face darkness outdoors at night (even most moonlit nights), within the confines of an unlit dungeon or a subterranean vault, or in an area of magical darkness.

Redirect Flow. You cause flowing water in the area to move in a direction you choose, even if the water has to flow over obstacles, up walls, or in other unlikely directions. The water in the area moves as you direct it, but once it moves beyond the spell's area, it resumes its flow based on the terrain conditions. The water continues to move in the direction you chose until the spell ends or you choose a different effect.
Whirlpool. This effect requires a body of water at least 50 feet square and 25 feet deep. You cause a whirlpool to form in the center of the area. The whirlpool forms a vortex that is 5 feet wide at the base, up to 50 feet wide at the top, and 25 feet tall. Any creature or object in the water and within 25 feet of the vortex is pulled 10 feet toward it. A creature can swim away from the vortex by making a Strength (Athletics) check against your spell save DC. When a creature enters the vortex for the first time on a turn or starts its turn there, it must make a Strength saving throw. On a failed save, the creature takes 2d8 bludgeoning damage and is caught in the vortex until the spell ends. On a successful save, the creature takes half damage, and isn't caught in the vortex. A creature caught in the vortex can use a standard action to try to swim away from the vortex as described above, but has disadvantage on the Strength (Athletics) check to do so.
The first time each turn that an object enters the vortex, the object takes 2d8 bludgeoning damage; this damage occurs each round it remains in the vortex.
