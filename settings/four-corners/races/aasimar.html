Aasimar

Aasimar

Aasimar can be born to any race. Barring magic (an aasimar's celestial nature can be detected even while it is still in the womb), there is no sure way to distiniguish an aasimar child from a normal child, though all aasimar are exceptionially beautiful and hale. Most aasimar first manifest their powers during adolescence.

Portentous Birth

An aasimar's birth is often marked by a good or ill omen, depending on whether the aasimar is theurgic or malefic. See the Aasimar Omens table for examples of omens. It is said that the magnitude of the omen is commensurate to the impact of the aasimar's life on the world.

Innate Alignment

Unlike other races, aasimar never deviate from their expected alignment: good for theurgic, or evil for malefic. Even tieflings can fight their impulses towards chaos or orderliness, but for aasimar, there is nothing to fight.

Game Mechanics

Aasimar

Abilities. Aasimar have the following ability score tendencies.
Inherited Attributes. Aasimar inherit the attributes of their parents, but often supernaturally enhanced.
Unearthly Beauty. Above other attributes, an aasimar's beauty is particularly enhanced, which often comes with a higher Charisma.
Alignment. Theurgic aasimar are invariably kind and beneficent, while malefic aasimar are apathetic and cruel. Upbringing might explain some variation in how good or evil an aasimar is, but there are no known exceptions to the overall rule.
Age. Aasimar are blessed with a longer lifespan than that of their parents. Your old age threshold and aging rate are 50 percent longer than those of your parents' race.
Inherited Traits. Your size, base walking speed, and languages are inherited from your parents' race, which can be any race of your choice that doesn't also have this trait. You also inherit any trait that grants a climb, swim, or fly speed, or the ability to breathe underwater.
Darkvision. Your enhanced vision gives you darkvision out to a range of 60 feet.
Celestial Soul. Your creature type is Humanoid, but you also register as a Celestial to abilities that detect celestials.

Theurgic Aasimar

Radiant Resistance. You have resistance to radiant damage.
Theurgic Spells. You know the light cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you learn the cure wounds spell. When you reach 5th level, you learn the calm emotions spell. You can cast each of these spells once with this trait and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells when you cast them with this trait (choose when you select this race), and you require no material components when you do so.
Theurgic Presence. Starting at 3rd level, you can use a standard action to unleash the divine energy within yourself, causing your eyes to glimmer and a luminous halo to surround you. You create an aura out to a range of 15 feet with the following effects:
  • You shed bright light within the aura, and dim light for an additional 15 feet outside the aura.
  • When you or a non-evil ally regains hit points while in the aura and not in darkness, it regains additional hit points equal to your proficiency bonus. A creature can receive this extra healing no more than once per turn.
  • You and your good-aligned allies have advantage on Wisdom saving throws while in the aura and not in darkness.
Your aura lasts for 1 minute. It ends early if you are knocked unconscious. You can also end it on your turn as a minor action.
You can activate your aura a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus. You regain expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Malefic Aasimar

Necrotic Resistance. You have resistance to necrotic damage.
Malefic Spells. You know the toll the dead cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you learn the cause fear spell. When you reach 5th level, you learn the darkness spell. You can cast each of these spells once with this trait and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells when you cast them with this trait (choose when you select this race), and you require no material components when you do so.
Malefic Presence. Starting at 3rd level, you can use a standard action to unleash the divine energy within yourself, causing your eyes to turn into pools of darkness and dreadful shadows to dance around you. You create an aura out to a range of 15 feet with the following effects:
  • Bright light within the aura turns to dim light.
  • When a creature other than you takes damage while in the aura and not in bright light, it takes additional necrotic damage equal to half your proficiency bonus. A creature can take this damage no more than once per turn.
  • You and your evil-aligned allies have advantage on Wisdom saving throws while in the aura and not in bright light.
Your aura lasts for 1 minute. It ends early if you are knocked unconscious. You can also end it on your turn as a minor action.
You can activate your aura a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus. You regain expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Suggested Feat

Searing Presence

Prerequisite: Aasimar, 3rd level
Cost: 8 feat points
When you activate your Theurgic or Malefic Presence, you can choose to augment it with the following additional effects:
  • Spectral wings grow from your back, granting you a fly speed of 40 feet. These wings are made of brilliant light (theurgic) or murky darkness (malefic). You choose their shape when you select this feat.
  • Once on each of your turns, you can deal extra damage to one target when you deal damage to it with an attack or a spell. The extra damage equals your proficiency bonus, and is radiant (theurgic) or necrotic (malefic).
  • You take damage at the start of each of your turns. The damage equals the number of times you have already used this feature to augment your Presence since you last completed a long rest, and its type is radiant (theurgic) or necrotic (malefic). The damage bypasses resistance.

Obscured

When vision in an area is hindered by smoke, darkness, or a similar phenomenon, the area is obscured. There are two degrees of obscurity.

Lightly Obscured

In a lightly obscured area, such as dim light, patchy fog, or moderate foliage, creatures have disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.

Heavily Obscured

A heavily obscured area, such as darkness, opaque fog, or dense foliage, blocks vision entirely. Nothing in that area can be seen.

light

Light

The presence or absence of light in an environment creates three categories of illumination: bright light, dim light, and darkness.

Bright Light

Bright light lets most creatures see normally. Even gloomy days provide bright light, as do torches, lanterns, fires, and other sources of illumination within a specific radius.

Dim Light

Dim light, also called shadows, creates a lightly obscured area. An area of dim light is usually a boundary between a source of bright light, such as a torch, and surrounding darkness. The soft light of twilight and dawn also counts as dim light. A particularly brilliant full moon might bathe the land in dim light.

Darkness

Darkness creates a heavily obscured area. Characters face darkness outdoors at night (even most moonlit nights), within the confines of an unlit dungeon or a subterranean vault, or in an area of magical darkness.

prone

Prone

While prone, you experience the following effects:
  • The only movement mode available to you is crawling.
  • You have disadvantage on attack rolls.
  • An attack roll against you has advantage if the attacker is within 5 feet of you. Otherwise, the attack roll has disadvantage.
You can end this condition by standing up, which requires spending half of your walk speed when you move.
This condition is commonly caused by falling, falling unconscious, and the trip attack.
