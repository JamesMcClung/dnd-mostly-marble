Tiefling

A tiefling, which translates roughly to "low one" in Sonnern, is a cursed being. A child of any race may be born a tiefling, and virtually any parent would abandon such a child as soon as the curse became evident. Tieflings are shunned by most people due to prejudices and fears—which may be justified—concerning them. It is well-known that tiefling behave erratically, and many people believe that their supernatural visage and powers mark them as evil.

The Curse

The Tiefling's Curse usually manifests as oddly colored skin, hair, and eyes, with the eyes being completely monochromatic. These traits may not appear until the tiefling child is a year old. In extreme cases, a tiefling can have horns, sharp teeth, claws instead of nails, slits for a nose, or even a tail; these traits appear much sooner, even before birth.
The true impact of the Curse is mental, not physical. A tiefling gets impulses to act either chaotically or orderly, and feels discomfort from resisting these impulses. Some tieflings even hear voices encouraging such behaviors. The Curse strengthens with age, both mentally and physically. When a tiefling fully succumbs to the Curse, they act solely on these impulses for few years before entering a coma and ultimately dying. The peak of corruption feat represents the exceptional tiefling whose Curse-effected deformities are beneficial.
Magic can remove the Curse and banish the impulses entirely. This comes at the cost of losing certain powers, but is often preferable to insanity.
It is possible to end the Curse in such a way that a tiefling gains powers instead of losing them. This happens extremely rarely, and apparently only to tieflings who embrace their impulses. The amalgamated chaos and amalgamated order feats represents this transition.

Game Mechanics

Tiefling

Abilities. Tieflings generally inherit the physical attributes of their parents. They often have rough childhoods and learn to be quick, cunning, or charismatic to survive.
Age. All tieflings eventually succomb to the Tiefling's Curse. Your old age threshold and aging rate are two-thirds those of your parents' race.
Alignment. Atrophic tiefling are supernaturally drawn to freedom and chaos, while leguleian tieflings are discomforted by lies and broken laws. The vicious upbringing of many tieflings may exacerbate these tendencies. Some tieflings resist, although it takes constant effort and gets more difficult with age.
Inherited Traits. Your size, base walking speed, and languages are inherited from your parents' race. You also inherit any trait that grants a climbing, swimming, or flying speed, or the ability to breathe underwater.
Darkvision. You have darkvision out to a range of 60 feet.
Fiendish Soul. Your creature type is Humanoid, but you also register as a Fiend to abilities that detect fiends.
Tiefling's Curse. You are cursed. The Curse manifests in the following ways, depending on your subrace:
  • You feel impulses towards chaos (Atrophic) or order (Leguleian).
  • You are discomforted when chaos (Atrophic) or order (Leguleian) is not upheld.
  • You have the Atrophic Spells or Leguleian Spells trait.
If remove curse is cast on you, the Curse is lifted from you for 1 minute, temporarily ending the above effects of the Curse. If remove curse was cast at a higher level, the Curse is lifted for longer: 1 day at 5th or 6th level, 1 month at 7th or 8th level, or 1 year at 9th level.

Atrophic Tiefling

Atrophic Resistance. You have resistance to necrotic damage.
Atrophic Spells. You know the thaumaturgy cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you learn the inflict wounds spell. When you reach 5th level, you learn the crown of madness spell. You can cast each of these spells once with this trait and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells when you cast them with this trait (choose when you select this race), and you require no material components when you do so.

Leguleian Tiefling

Leguleian Resistance. You have resistance to fire damage.
Leguleian Spells. You know the thaumaturgy cantrip. When you reach 3rd level, you learn the hellish rebuke spell. When you reach 5th level, you learn the zone of truth spell. You can cast each of these spells once with this trait and regain the ability to do so when you finish a long rest. Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for these spells when you cast them with this trait (choose when you select this race), and you require no material components when you do so.

Suggested Feats

Amalgamated Chaos

Prerequisite: Tiefling (atrophic), 3rd level and chaotic alignment
Cost: 10 feat points
You have accepted—or succombed to—the urges in your head. You gain the following benefits:
  • You lose your Tiefling's Curse trait, but you retain your Atrophic Spells trait.
  • For you, thaumaturgy has a range of 150 feet, a duration of 10 minutes, and can be cast as a minor action.
  • You can use Atrophic Spells to cast inflict wounds a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, instead of once, per long rest.
  • You can cast withering wounds instead of hellish rebuke with your Atrophic Spells trait.
  • You can cast court of madness instead of crown of madness with your Atrophic Spells trait.

Amalgamated Order

Prerequisite: Tiefling (leguleian), 3rd level and lawful alignment
Cost: 10 feat points
You have accepted—or succombed to—the urges in your head. You gain the following benefits:
  • You lose your Tiefling's Curse trait, but you retain your Leguleian Spells trait.
  • For you, thaumaturgy has a range of 150 feet, a duration of 10 minutes, and can be cast as a minor action.
  • You can use Leguleian Spells to cast hellish rebuke a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, instead of once, per long rest.
  • You can cast hellish interdiction instead of hellish rebuke with your Leguleian Spells trait.
  • You can cast zone of command instead of zone of truth with your Leguleian Spells trait.

Peak of Corruption

Prerequisite: Tiefling, Tiefling's Curse trait
Cost: 8 feat points
Your Curse warps your body and mind beyond what is normally survivable. Your Tiefling's Curse manifests in an additional way: you have the indefinite madness, "sometimes, the voice in my head takes control."
When remove curse is cast on you, you must make a Constitution saving throw against the spell save DC. On a success, your Tiefling's Curse is not lifted.
Finally, choose one of the following benefits:
  1. You grow bat-like wings, granting you a flying speed of 30 feet. This flying speed is halved while you wear heavy armor.
  2. Your skin hardens to chitinous or osseous plates. Your base AC can be calculated as 10 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution modifier, regardless of your armor.
  3. Your tail, horns, and/or claws are empowered by your Curse and you can use them to make unarmed strikes. They count as magical natural weapons with the finesse and light properties, have a damage die of 1d8, and deal bludgeoning damage (tail), piercing damage (horns), or slashing damage (claws). Once on each of your turns, you can cause a creature hit by one of your unarmed strikes to take an extra 1d8 fire damage (Leguleian tiefling) or necrotic damage (Atrophic tiefling).
You can take this feat multiple times. Each time you do, choose a different benefit.

Thaumaturgy

Transmutation cantrip
Components
V
Duration
1 minute
Casting Time
1 standard action
Range
30 feet
You manifest a minor wonder, a sign of supernatural power, within range. You create one of the following magical effects within range.
  • Your voice booms up to three times as loud as normal for 1 minute.
  • You cause flames to flicker, brighten, dim, or change color for 1 minute.
  • You cause harmless tremors in the ground for 1 minute.
  • You create an instantaneous sound that originates from a point of your choice within range, such as a rumble of thunder, the cry of a raven, or ominous whispers.
  • You instantaneously cause an unlocked door or window to fly open or slam shut.
  • You alter the appearance of your eyes for 1 minute.
If you cast this spell multiple times, you can have up to three of its 1-minute effects active at a time, and you can dismiss such an effect as a standard action.

V, S

Casting Time
1 standard action
Range
60 feet

command

Command

1st-level enchantment
Components
V
Duration
1 round
Casting Time
1 standard action
Range
60 feet
You speak a one-word command to a creature you can see within range. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or follow the command on its next turn. The spell has no effect if the target is undead, if it doesn't understand your language, or if your command is directly harmful to it.
Some typical commands and their effects follow. You might issue a command other than one described here. If you do so, the DM determines how the target behaves. If the target can't follow your command, the spell ends.
Approach. The target moves toward you by the shortest and most direct route, ending its turn if it moves within 5 feet of you.
Drop. The target drops whatever it is holding and then ends its turn.
Flee. The target spends its turn moving away from you by the fastest available means.
Grovel. The target falls prone and then ends its turn.
Halt. The target doesn't move and takes no actions. A flying creature stays aloft, provided that it is able to do so. If it must move to stay aloft, it flies the minimum distance needed to remain in the air.
At Higher Levels. When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 2nd level or higher, you can affect one additional creature for each slot level above 1st. The creatures must be within 30 feet of each other when you target them.
Available To
Bard, cleric, paladin
