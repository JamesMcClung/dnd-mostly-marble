Dragonborn

Dragonborn

  • Inhabitants of Dragon Island, along with Kobolds
  • Two group: metallic and chromatic
  • Each group reveres respective dragons, which are technically their ancestors
  • "Ancient Ones" were dragonborn who lived for centuries, and grew to huge sizes

Game Mechanics

Dragonborn

Abilities. Dragonborn are often very strong and zealous, corresponding to high Strength and Charisma. More primal or reserved dragonborn may have sharper senses, corresponding to Wisdom.
Age. Dragonbornlings spend 6 months in an egg before hatching. Youths reach maturity at age 12 when they are 4 to 5 feet tall, but continue to grow their entire lives. Their wings typically develop around age 25. At old ages, their still-growing size makes them increasingly vulnerable to heart failure. Your old age threshold is 70 years, with an aging rate of 1 year.
Alignment. On Dragon Island, dragonborn of both creeds follow the examples set by their draconic forefathers. Metallic dragonborn on the island are intensely righteous and even callous, but never malicious. On the other hand, the chromatic dragonborn are egotistical, greedy, and ruled by strength.
Away from Dragon Island, these ideological trends are significantly less pronounced. Whatever an individual dragonborn's outlook, it is almost surely in the extreme; the only alignment that is rare in dragonborn is true neutrality.
Languages. You can speak, read, and write Draconic. Dragonborn are generally monolingual on Dragon Island, and most dragonborn who leave the island still retain their native tongue.
Size. Your size is Medium. Adult dragonborn usually reach 6 feet tall within 20 years, after which their growth starts to slow. Even so, elder dragonborn often exceed 8 feet in height. Dragonborn also grow heavier throughout their life, with adults ranging from 200 to 400 pounds on average as they age.
Speed. Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
Darkvision. Inheriting keen eyes from dragons, you have darkvision out to a range of 60 feet.
Draconic Ancestry. Your draconic heritage manifests in several ways based on your draconic hue. Pick one dragon type from the Draconic Ancestry table. Your choice affects your Breath Weapon and Damage Resistance traits.

Breath Weapon. You can accumulate elemental energy within yourself and unleash it with your breath. You have a number of breath weapon dice equal to 2 + half your level, which are d6s. As a minor action, you can roll and expend any number of these dice and force each creature in your breath weapon area to make a saving throw. The shape, saving throw, and damage type are determined by your Draconic Ancestry. The DC is 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength or Constitution modifier (your choice). A creature takes damage equal the number rolled on the expended dice on a failed save, or half as much on a success.
You regain all expended breath weapon dice at the end of a short or long rest.
Damage Resistance. You have resistance to the damage type associated with your Draconic Ancestry.

Suggested Feats

Shielding Wings

Prerequisite: Dragonborn
Cost: 8 feat points
Your bulk makes flying difficult, but your tough wings make excellent shields. You gain the following benefits:
  • Your wings count as a kite shield, and you are proficient with them.
  • Your base AC can be calculated as 10 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength modifier, regardless of your armor.
  • If you also have the Shield Master feat, you can use the minor action to shove each creature in a 15-foot cube instead of just one creature if you do so with your wings.

Soaring Wings

Prerequisite: Dragonborn
Cost: 8 feat points
Your wings are strong enough for flight. You gain a flying speed of 40 feet. If you also have the Shielding Wings feat or are wearing heavy armor, this flying speed is halved.

Shield Master

Shield Master

Cost: 8 feat points
You use shields not just for protection but also for offense. You gain the following benefits while wielding a shield that you are proficient with:
  • If you take the Attack action on your turn, you can use a minor action to try to shove a creature within 5 feet of you with your shield.
  • If you aren't incapacitated, you can add your shield's AC bonus to any Dexterity saving throw you make against a spell or other harmful effect that targets only you.
  • If you are subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, you can use your reaction to take no damage if you succeed on the saving throw, interposing your shield between yourself and the source of the effect.

Prone

While prone, you experience the following effects:
  • Your only movement option is to crawl, unless you stand up and thereby end the condition.
  • You have disadvantage on attack rolls.
  • An attack roll against you has advantage if the attacker is within 5 feet of you. Otherwise, the attack roll has disadvantage.
Standing up costs an amount of movement equal to half your current speed. You can't stand up if you don't have enough movement left or if your speed is 0.
This condition is commonly caused by falling, falling unconscious, and the trip attack.

Flying lets you move through the air. Doing so requires the use of a flying speed.
