Humans

Humans are native to Ata, where they compete with Orcs for control over the plains.

  • Biggest seafaring race by far. Hobgoblins are the only other race to even have seaworthy vessels.
  • Even before sea travel was possible, human mages traveled the world and facilitated cultural exchange between Humans, Dwarves, and Elves.
  • Humans have established at least one colony on all three continents, plus one settlement on the Ice Plains and several outposts in the Pyrmies.
  • Many ethnicities exist, but the two biggest are the Etelaan and the Sonnern.
    • Etelaan: bronze-skinned, black-haired, and brown-eyed southwesterners who currently control most of the south and west
      • adjective=Etelaan, region=Etela
      • shorter on average
      • exceptionally collaborative, building a strong network of schools, hospitals, and libraries
    • Sonnern: light/tan-skinned, dirty-blond-haired, and blue-eyed easterners who currently control the east coast
      • adjective=Sonnern, region=Land der Sonne ("Land of the Sun")
      • militaristic, ruled by merchant-lords controlling armies of mercenaries
      • worship the Sun, observing its rise every day
      • originally from northeast of Ata
    • Tenga: dark-skinned, bald, amber-eyed plainsmen
      • adjective=Tengan, people=Tenga, region=Tanatenga
      • once very powerful and responsible for many ruins across the plains
      • decimated after bad earthquakes ruined their defenses and orcs invaded
      • continue to live in ruins, trying to relearn lost secrets
    • Fune: brown-skinned, black/gray-haired, dark-eyed southerners
      • adjective=Funish, people=Fune, region=Funetochi
      • taller on average
      • the original seafarers, but Etelaan and Sonnern copied them
      • chose to be nomadic seafarers (and/or pirates) rather than be Etelaan subjects
      • primarily sail in the Pyrmies
    • Shamal: light-skinned, dark-brown-haired, blue-eyed northerners
      • adjective=Shamalian, people=Shamal, region=Shamalia
      • rarely have red hair, which is taken as a blessing from the gods
      • pushed out of northlands by Orcs, and now live in the fringes of Sonnern territory
      • each tribe is led by its priestess, who in turn serve the High Priestess
        • gods manifest as animals
    • Ogof: pale-skinned, blond/white-haired, and red-eyed cave dwellers in the mountains
      • adjective=Ogofen, people=Ogof, region=Tir Ogof
      • can't stand bright light, especially sunlight
      • persecuted by Sonnern, now only isolated tribes remain in the east
      • a great Ogof city is rumored to exist in the western mountains
      • different from other humans due to isolation in caves and some sort of magical influence
        • adapted for darkness and more magically adept
      • music is very important to them
        • long, low notes (especially throat singing) that reverberate in caves
        • gods respond in the echoes
        • can broadcast messages throughout entire underground using powerful drums built into the ground

Game Mechanics

Human

Abilities. Humans are the most diverse race. Some groups may hone one or more particular abilities, but there's no telling what a random human is good at.

Age. Humans reach adulthood at around 20 and old age at 70 years. Few live longer than a century.

Alignment. Humans generally share an alignment with their peers, which in turn varies across cultures and generations. A human can easily take on any alignment.

Size. Your size is Medium.

Speed. Your base walking speed is 30 feet.

Languages. You can speak, read, and write the language of your upbringing and one other language of your choice.

Common Human

Personal Talent. Humans have a knack for picking up skills that may not even be useful to them. You gain proficiency in one skill or tool of your choice. At 3rd level, you gain expertise in that skill or tool.

Personal Disposition. Choose one of the following benefits:

  • Collaborative. When you use the Help action to give a creature advantage on an ability check, the creature can add 1d4 to the check (or 1d6 if your Personal Talent would apply to the check). If the check fails, that creature cannot benefit from this feature again until it finishes a long rest.
  • Aggressive. When you fail an ability check, you can add 1d4 to the roll (or 1d6 if your Personal Talent applied to the check), potentially turning the failure into a success. However, if you ever fail a check that your Personal Talent applies to or a check that you used this feature on, you cannot use this feature again until you finish a long rest.
  • Stubborn. When you make an ability check, you can add 1d4 to the roll (or 1d6 if your Personal Talent applies to the check). If the check succeeds, you cannot use this feature again until you finish a long rest.

Worldly. You can pick an additional background.

Ogof

Darkvision. Your unusually large eyes confer darkvision out to a range of 60 ft.

Sunlight Sensitivity. You have disadvantage on attack rolls and on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight when you, the target of your attack, or whatever you are trying to perceive is in direct sunlight.

Soulsight. You can sense the presence and location of magic within 30 feet of you when you are not incapacitated. This ability cannot penetrate through 3 feet of wood or dirt, 1 foot of stone, 1 inch of common metal, or a thin sheet of lead.

As an action, you can focus your attention on magic you can sense in this way. If you do so, you magically see a faint aura outlining the shape of the creature, object, or area bearing the magic, and you learn its school of magic, if any. You can use this action a number of times equal to your proficiency bonus, regaining expended uses after a long rest.

Overchannel. Once when you cast a spell using a spell slot of 5th level or lower, you can can increase the level of the spell by 1. You regain the use of this feature when you finish a short or long rest.
