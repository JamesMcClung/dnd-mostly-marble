Class Features

Hit Points

Hit Dice: 1d8 per monk level
Hit Points at 1st Level: 8 + your Constitution modifier
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per monk level after 1st

Proficiencies

Armor: None
Weapons: Simple weapons, shortswords
Tools: One type of artisan's tools or musical instrument of your choice
Saving Throws: Choose two from Strength, Dexterity, and Wisdom
Skills: Choose two from Acrobatics, Athletics, History, Insight, Religion, and Stealth

Multiclassing

Ability Score Minimum: Dexterity 13 and Wisdom 13
Proficiencies Gained: Simple weapons, shortswords

Equipment

You start with the following equipment:
  • (a) a shortsword or (b) any simple weapon
  • (a) a dungeoneer's pack or (b) an explorer's pack
  • 10 darts
Alternatively, you can forgo the equipment here and buy 5d4 gp worth of equipment.

Unarmored Defense

1st-level Monk feature
While you aren't wearing armor or wielding a shield, your base AC can be calculated as 10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Wisdom modifier.

Martial Arts

1st-level Monk feature
Your practice of martial arts gives you mastery of combat styles that use monk weapons, which are shortswords and any simple melee weapons (including unarmed attacks) that don't have the two-handed or heavy property.
You gain the following benefits while you are wielding only monk weapons and you aren't wearing armor or wielding a shield:
  • You can use Dexterity instead of Strength for the attack and damage rolls of your monk weapons.
  • When you roll damage for a monk weapon, you can replace the weapon's damage die with your Martial Arts die. This die starts as a d4 and changes as you gain monk levels, as shown in the Martial Arts Die column of the Monk table.
  • You add your ability modifier to the damage of unarmed strikes you make with the Offhand Attack action.
Certain monasteries use specialized forms of monk weapons. For example, you might use a club that is two lengths of wood connected by a short chain (called a nunchaku) or a sickle with a shorter, straighter blade (called a kama). Whatever name you use for a monk weapon, you can use its game statistics in the weapons table.

Monk Distance

2nd-level Monk feature
You have a Monk Distance, which is 10 feet. This distance increases as you gain levels as a monk, as shown in the Monk table. Several other monk features refer to this distance.

Unarmored Movement

2nd-level Monk feature
You gain a bonus to speed equal to your Monk Distance while you are not wearing armor or wielding a shield.
At 9th level, you gain the ability to move along vertical surfaces and across liquids on your turn without falling during the move.

Stances

2nd-level Monk feature
You flow through a variety of stances in combat. Stances allow you to channel a mystical energy called ki that supernaturally enhances your abilities.
As a free action, you can adopt a stance until the start of your next turn. You can't adopt the same stance on consecutive turns. A stance ends early if you die, are incapacitated, or choose to end it (no action required). You also don't gain the benefits of a stance while you are wearing armor or wielding a shield.
You start with three stances: Flurry of Blows, Patient Defense, and Step of the Wind.
Some of your stances require your target to make a saving throw to resist an effect. The saving throw DC is calculated as follows:
Stance save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier

Flurry of Blows

When you take the Offhand Attack action on your turn, you can make two unarmed strikes instead of one.

Patient Defense

You gain blindsight with a range equal to your Monk Distance. Additionally, the Dodge action only costs 1 AP for you.

Step of the Wind

Your movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks, and your speed and jump distance both gain a bonus equal to your Monk Distance.

Dedicated Weapon

2nd-level Monk feature
You train yourself to use a variety of weapons as monk weapons, not just simple melee weapons and shortswords. Whenever you finish a short or long rest, you can touch one weapon, focus your ki on it, and then count that weapon as a monk weapon until you use this feature again.
The chosen weapon must meet these criteria:
  • The weapon must be a simple or martial weapon.
  • You must be proficient with it.
  • It can't have the heavy property or any special properties.

Deflect Missiles

3rd-level Monk feature
You can use your reaction to deflect or catch the missile when you are hit by a ranged weapon attack. When you do so, the damage you take from the attack is reduced by 1d10 + your Dexterity modifier + your Monk level. If you are in the Patient Defense stance, you gain this benefit without using your reaction, provided nothing is preventing you from taking reactions.
If you reduce the damage to 0, you can catch the missile if it is small enough for you to hold in one hand and you have at least one hand free. If you are in the Patient Defense stance, you can then make a ranged attack with the weapon or piece of ammunition you just caught as a reaction. You make this attack with proficiency, regardless of your weapon proficiencies, and the missile counts as a monk weapon with the thrown (range 20/60) property for the attack.

Monastic Tradition

3rd-level Monk feature
You commit yourself to a monastic tradition. Your tradition grants you features at 3rd level and again at 6th, 11th, and 17th level.
Way of the Four Elements
Source: Four Corners
A monk following the Way of the Four Elements enhances their stances with the unique attributes of air, earth, fire, and water.

Elemental Stances

3rd-level Way of the Four Elements feature
You can imbue your stances with elemental power. When you adopt a stance, choose Air, Earth, Fire or Water. Your choice gives you benefits for the duration of the stance. You can't choose the same element on consecutive turns.

Air

You magically gain a hover speed equal to your Monk Distance.
If you are in the Step of the Wind stance, this hover speed is doubled.

Earth

You gain tremorsense with a range equal to your Monk Distance. If you are standing on earth or stone and are hit by an attack you can see, you can use your reaction to magically protect yourself with an earthen shield. You gain half cover against the attack, potentially causing the attack to miss.
If you are in the Patient Defense stance, the range of this tremorsense is doubled and the reaction gives three-quarters cover instead of half cover.

Fire

When you would make an unarmed strike, you can replace it with a special ranged spell attack. The attack has a range equal to your Monk Distance and uses your Martial Arts die as its damage die. You are proficient with it, and add your Dexterity modifier to the attack and damage roll.
If you are in the Flurry of Blows stance, the range of this attack is doubled, and on your turn you can add an additional Martial Arts die to the damage roll of one of these attacks.

Water

You magically gain a swim speed equal to twice your Monk Distance. As a standard action, you can end your stance and transfer water's lifegiving energy to a creature you touch, which can be yourself. The creature magically regains hit points equal to a roll of your Martial Arts die plus your Wisdom modifier (minimum 0). This action has no effect on undead and constructs, and once a creature regains hit points in this way, it can't do so again until it finishes a long rest.

Elemental Deflection

6th-level Way of the Four Elements feature
When you are in an elemental stance and take damage from your element, you can use your reaction to take half damage instead. After you take this reaction, the element ends for your stance.
The Elemental Damage table shows what damage you can reduce with this feature. For example, you can reduce the bludgeoning damage from a wind wall while in an Air stance, or the piercing damage from an ice knife while in a Water stance.

Elemental Eruption

11th-level Way of the Four Elements feature
When you are in an elemental stance, you can concentrate all of the stance's energy into a burst of power. You cast a spell of your choice from the options shown in the Elemental Eruption Spells table for your current stance. Your spellcasting ability is Wisdom for the spell, and you don't need to provide any components.
If the spell requires concentration, your stance doesn't end. Instead, you keep your current element until your concentration ends, even as you change stances. You lose concentration if your stance ends and you don't immediately adopt a new stance.
Immediately after you lose concentration on the spell, or after casting the spell if it didn't require concentration, your stance ends and you can't imbue a stance with that element again until you finish a long rest.

Elemental Harmony

17th-level Way of the Four Elements feature
You have learned to harmonize the elements. When you adopt a stance, you can imbue it with any combination of elements. This doesn't allow you to select the same element on consecutive turns, however.

Ability Score Improvement

4th-level Monk feature
When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can't increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

Slow Fall

4th-level Monk feature
You can use your reaction when you fall to reduce any falling damage you take by an amount equal to five times your monk level. If you're in the Step of the Wind stance, you can gain this benefit without using a reaction, provided nothing is preventing you from taking reactions.

Quickened Healing

4th-level Monk feature
As a minor action, you can roll a Martial Arts die and regain a number of hit points equal to the number rolled plus your Wisdom modifier (minimum 0).
Once you use this action, you can't use it again until you finish a short or long rest.

Extra Attack

5th-level Monk feature
When you take the Attack action on your turn, you can make two attacks instead of one.

Stunning Strikes

5th-level Monk feature
You can interfere with the flow of ki in an opponent's body. When you are in the Flurry of Blows stance and hit another creature with a melee weapon attack, the target must succeed on a Constitution saving throw or be dazed until the end of your next turn. If the target failed the save by 10 or more, it is stunned instead of dazed.

Sweeping Wind

6th-level Monk feature
When you are in the Step of the Wind stance and miss with an attack roll, you can immediately end the stance to increase your attack roll by 5, potentially turning the miss into a hit.

Evasion

7th-level Monk feature
Your instinctive agility lets you dodge out of the way of certain area effects, such as a blue dragon's lightning breath or a fireball spell. When you are subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, you instead take no damage if you succeed on the saving throw, and only half damage if you fail.

Stillness of Mind

7th-level Monk feature
You can use a standard action to end one effect on yourself that is causing you to be charmed, taunted, or frightened.

Purity of Body

10th-level Monk feature
Your mastery of the ki flowing through you makes you immune to disease, poison damage, and the poisoned condition.

Tongue of Sun and Moon

13th-level Monk feature
You learn to touch the ki of other minds so that you can understand all spoken languages. Moreover, any creature that can understand a language can understand what you say.

Diamond Soul

14th-level Monk feature
Your mastery of ki grants you proficiency in all saving throws.
Additionally, you ignore the effects of the dazed, stunned, and paralyzed conditions on your turn.

Timeless Body

15th-level Monk feature
Your ki sustains you so that you suffer none of the penalties of exhaustion due to old age, and you can't be aged magically. You can still die from the exhaustion, however. In addition, you no longer need food or water.

Empty Body

18th-level Monk feature
You gain a new stance: Empty Body. In this stance, you are invisible and have resistance to all damage but force damage.
If you are in the Empty Body stance, you can use a major action to end the stance and cast astral projection, without needing material components. You can't take any other creatures with you. Once you use this action, you can't adopt the Empty Body stance again until you finish a long rest.

Perfect Self

20th-level Monk feature
You can adopt the Flurry of Blows, Patient Defense, and Step of the Wind stances on consecutive turns.

fall

Falling

When you begin falling, you immediately fall 60 feet. While falling, you drop another 180 feet at the start of each of your turns.
On impact, you take one size die of bludgeoning damage for every 10 feet you fell, to a maximum of 20 dice. You land prone, unless you avoid taking damage from the fall.
You can use your reaction to mitigate the damage from landing. When you do so, make an Acrobatics check and reduce the fall damage by half the result.
If you land in a creature's space, that creature must make a DC 10 Dexterity saving throw. It can choose to fail. On a failed save, the fall damage is split evenly between you and it.

A creature with tremorsense can pinpoint the location of creatures and moving objects within a specific range, provided that the creature with tremorsense and anything it's detecting are both in contact with the same surface (such as the ground, a wall, or a ceiling) or the same liquid.

cover

Cover

Walls, trees, creatures, and other obstacles can provide cover during combat, making a target more difficult to harm. A target can benefit from cover only when an attack or other effect originates on the opposite side of the cover.
There are three degrees of cover. If a target is behind multiple sources of cover, only the most protective degree of cover applies; the degrees aren't added together. For example, if a target is behind a creature that gives half cover and a tree trunk that gives three-quarters cover, the target has three-quarters cover.

Half Cover

A target with half cover has a +2 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws. A target has half cover if an obstacle blocks at least half of its body. The obstacle might be a low wall, a large piece of furniture, a narrow tree trunk, or a creature, whether that creature is an enemy or a friend.

Three-Quarters Cover

A target with three-quarters cover has a +5 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws. A target has three-quarters cover if about three-quarters of it is covered by an obstacle. The obstacle might be a portcullis, an arrow slit, or a thick tree trunk.

Total Cover

A target with total cover can't be targeted directly by an attack or a spell, although some spells can reach such a target by including it in an area of effect. A target has total cover if it is completely concealed by an obstacle.

Jumping

Jumping

Jumping is a movement modifier that lets you clear gaps or obstacles. Instead of the usual movement cost, you must spend 1 foot of movement for every foot you jump vertically or horizontally (whichever is higher).
When you jump, perform the following series of calculations; don't round until the end. The resulting value is the maximum number of feet you can jump.
  1. Start with your passive Athletics. Alternatively, you can make a Strength (Athletics check) and use the result instead.
  2. Divide the value by 2.
  3. If you did not get a running start immediately before jumping, divide the value by 2 again. To get a running start, you must move in a straight line in the direction you want to jump for a number of feet determined by your size, as shown in the Running Start Distances table. The speed you get a running start with must be the same as the speed you use to jump, and it must be with a natural movement mode.
  4. To determine the vertical distance you can clear, divide the value by 2 again.
If you land in difficult terrain, you must succeed on a DC 10 Dexterity (Acrobatics) check or fall prone.

obscured

Obscured

When vision in an area is hindered by smoke, darkness, or a similar phenomenon, the area is obscured. There are two degrees of obscurity.

Lightly Obscured

In a lightly obscured area, such as dim light, patchy fog, or moderate foliage, creatures have disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.

Heavily Obscured

A heavily obscured area, such as darkness, opaque fog, or dense foliage, blocks vision entirely. Nothing in that area can be seen.

light

Light

The presence or absence of light in an environment creates three categories of illumination: bright light, dim light, and darkness.

Bright Light

Bright light lets most creatures see normally. Even gloomy days provide bright light, as do torches, lanterns, fires, and other sources of illumination within a specific radius.

Dim Light

Dim light, also called shadows, creates a lightly obscured area. An area of dim light is usually a boundary between a source of bright light, such as a torch, and surrounding darkness. The soft light of twilight and dawn also counts as dim light. A particularly brilliant full moon might bathe the land in dim light.

Darkness

Darkness creates a heavily obscured area. Characters face darkness outdoors at night (even most moonlit nights), within the confines of an unlit dungeon or a subterranean vault, or in an area of magical darkness.

Astral Projection

9th-level necromancy
Components
V, S, M (for each creature you affect with this spell, you must provide one jacinth worth at least 1,000 gp and one ornately carved bar of silver worth at least 100 gp, all of which the spell consumes)
Duration
Special
Casting Time
1 hour
Range
10 feet
You and up to eight willing creatures within range project your astral bodies into the Astral Plane (the spell fails and the casting is wasted if you are already on that plane). The material body you leave behind is unconscious and in a state of suspended animation; it doesn't need food or air and doesn't age.
Your astral body resembles your mortal form in almost every way, replicating your game statistics and possessions. The principal difference is the addition of a silvery cord that extends from between your shoulder blades and trails behind you, fading to invisibility after 1 foot. This cord is your tether to your material body. As long as the tether remains intact, you can find your way home. If the cord is cut—something that can happen only when an effect specifically states that it does—your soul and body are separated, killing you instantly.
Your astral form can freely travel through the Astral Plane and can pass through portals there leading to any other plane. If you enter a new plane or return to the plane you were on when casting this spell, your body and possessions are transported along the silver cord, allowing you to re-enter your body as you enter the new plane. Your astral form is a separate incarnation. Any damage or other effects that apply to it have no effect on your physical body, nor do they persist when you return to it.
The spell ends for you and your companions when you use a standard action to dismiss it. When the spell ends, the affected creature returns to its physical body, and it awakens.
The spell might also end early for you or one of your companions. A successful dispel magic spell used against an astral or physical body ends the spell for that creature. If a creature's original body or its astral form drops to 0 hit points, the spell ends for that creature. If the spell ends and the silver cord is intact, the cord pulls the creature's astral form back to its body, ending its state of suspended animation.
If you are returned to your body prematurely, your companions remain in their astral forms and must find their own way back to their bodies, usually by dropping to 0 hit points.

Cleric, warlock, wizard
