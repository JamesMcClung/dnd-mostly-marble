Equipment Properties

Weapon Properties

Ammunition

You can use a weapon that has the ammunition property to make a ranged attack only if you have ammunition to fire from the weapon. Each time you attack with the weapon, you expend one piece of ammunition. Drawing the ammunition from a quiver, case, or other container is part of the attack. At the end of the battle, you can recover half your expended ammunition by taking a minute to search the battlefield.
If you use a weapon that has the ammunition property to make a melee attack, you treat the weapon as an improvised weapon. A sling must be loaded to deal any damage when used in this way.

Finesse

When making an attack with a finesse weapon, you use your choice of your Strength or Dexterity modifier for the attack and damage rolls. You must use the same modifier for both rolls.

Heavy

Heavy weapons hit harder, but can be difficult to wield. When you score a critical hit with a heavy weapon, you can add an additional weapon damage die to the damage roll. However, Small creaturesExcept those with the Powerful Build feat or trait have disadvantage on attack rolls with heavy weapons.

Hydrodynamic

A hydrodynamic weapon functions normally underwater.

Light

A light weapon is small and easy to handle, making it ideal for use when fighting with two weapons. You can take the Offhand Attack action to make an attack with a light weapon.

Range

A weapon that can be used to make a ranged attack has a range shown in parentheses after the "ammunition" or "thrown" property. The range lists two numbers. The first is the weapon's normal range in feet, and the second indicates the weapon's maximum range. When attacking a target beyond normal range, you have disadvantage on the attack roll. You can't attack a target beyond the weapon's long range.

Reach

A reach weapon adds 5 feet to your reach when you attack with it.

Thrown

If a weapon has the thrown property, you can throw the weapon to make a ranged attack. If the weapon is a melee weapon, you use the same ability modifier for that attack roll and damage roll that you would use for a melee attack with the weapon.
For example, if you throw a handaxe, you use your Strength, but if you throw a dagger, you can use either your Strength or your Dexterity, since the dagger has the finesse property.

Two-Handed

A two-handed weapon requires two hands to use.

Unarmed

You use your own arms, legs, or other suitable appendages as weapons to make an attack with an unarmed weapon.

Versatile

A versatile weapon can be used with one or two hands. A damage value in parentheses appears with the property—the damage when the weapon is used with two hands to make a melee attack.

Armor Properties

Cumbersome

Heavier armor interferes with the wearer's ability to move quickly. Armor with the cumbersome property reduces the wearer's speed by 10 feet unless the wearer has a Strength score equal to or higher than the score in parentheses.

Unstealthy

The wearer has disadvantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks.

Offhand Attack

Offhand Attack

Cost: 1 AP (nonatomic)
When you take the Offhand Attack action, you make one weapon attack with a light weapon. You don't add your ability modifier to the damage of the attack, unless that modifier is negative. Your other hand must be empty or holding another light weapon, and you can't use the Attack and Offhand Attack actions to attack with the same weapon on a turn.
