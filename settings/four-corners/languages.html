Languages

Gnomish

  • Many different variants and dialects

Gabrorian Gnomish

  • Different dialect in each rock gnome city, but all mutually intelligible
    • Rot gnomes in Tiris Vale can barely be understood
    • Significant Dwarvish influence, especially pertaining to geology and metallurgy

Not all gnomes in Gabroria speak Gabrorian Gnomish. Exceptions:

  • Sand gnomes speak similar but distinct language
    • Very little Dwarvish influence
  • Forest gnomes in Gabroria seemingly speak their own dialect of Fey Gnomish

Verdanian Gnomish

  • Only spoken by forest gnomes, with more extreme differences in dialect across regional gaps
  • Very different from Gabrorian varieties
    • Some Elvish and even Sylvan influence, but no Dwarvish influence

Fey Gnomish

  • Fewer dialects than Gabrorian or Verdanian variants
  • Seems to be somewhere in between Gabrorian and Verdanian
    • Only Sylvan influences- no Dwarvish or Elvish
    • Closer to Verdanian

Dwarvish

  • Many dialects, but all mutually intelligible
    • Dwarves rigorously maintain language conventions
  • Some dialects have more Gnomish influence

Elvish

  • Also refered to as Old Elvish
    • Writing system of Old Elvish uses topologically distinct characters
    • New writing system adapted from Sonnern to facilitate communication, especially in academia
  • Largely unchanging due to immortality of elves
    • Some recent loanwords from Sonnern language
  • Can sometimes tell generation of elf based on speech patterns

Marese

  • Spoken by Maredrin
  • Branched long ago from Elvish, no longer remotely intelligible
  • Influenced by Merrish and Triton
  • Malese dialect spoken by Maledrin
    • Influenced by kuo-toa, merrow, and other worshippers of dead kraken Lolugo

High Sylvan

  • Spoken mostly by Eladrin in court of Feywild
  • Branched long ago from Elvish, no longer remotely intelligible
  • Influenced slightly by other fey creatures

Sylvan

  • Spoken by denizens of Feywild
  • Vulgarization of High Sylvan
