Spells

Below are new spells available in this setting. If a spell is not on any spell list, it cannot be obtained via any means except when specifically granted. For example, the only way to cast withering wounds is if you are a tiefling with the Amalgamated Chaos feat.

Hellish Interdiction

1st-level abjuration
Components
V
Duration
1 round
Casting Time
1 reaction, which you take in response to being damaged by a creature within 60 feet of you that you can see
Range
60 feet
With a cold glare, hellish flames burst from the creature that damaged you. The creature must make a Charisma saving throw. It takes 2d10 fire damage on a failed save, or half as much damage on a successful one. On a failed save, the creature also cannot attack you or target you with harmful abilities or magical effects until the start of your next turn.

Zone of Command

2nd-level enchantment
concentration
Components
V, S
Duration
Up to 10 minutes
Casting Time
1 action
Range
60 feet
You create a magical zone to enforce your authority in a 10-foot-radius sphere centered on a point of your choice within range. Until the spell ends, a creature that enters the spell's area for the first time on a turn or starts its turn there must make a Charisma saving throw. On a failed save, a creature can't speak a deliberate lie while in the radius. You know whether each creature succeeds or fails on its saving throw.
An affected creature is aware of the spell and can thus avoid answering questions to which it would normally respond with a lie. Such a creature can be evasive in its answers as long as it remains within the boundaries of the truth.
Addtionally, you can cast command at will targeting any number of creatures within the zone.

Withering Wounds

1st-level necromancy
Components
S
Duration
Instantaneous
Casting Time
1 action
Range
Touch
Make a melee spell attack against a creature you can reach. On a hit, the target takes 3d10 necrotic damage and begins to wither.
While withering, the target must succeed on a Constitution saving throw at the end of each of its turns or take 1d10 necrotic damage. The withering ends on a successful save or after 1 minute. The spell also ends early if remove curse is cast on the target.

Court of Madness

2nd-level enchantment
concentration
Components
V, S
Duration
Up to 1 minute
Casting Time
1 action
Range
30 feet
A number of humanoids up to your proficiency bonus of your choice that you can see within range must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or become charmed by you for the duration. While a target is charmed in this way, a twisted crown of jagged iron appears on its head, and a madness glows in its eyes.
A charmed target must use its action before moving on each of its turns to make a melee attack against a creature other than itself that you mentally choose.
A target can act normally on its turn if you choose no creature or if none are within its reach.
On your subsequent turns, you must use your action or bonus action to maintain control over the targets, or the spell ends. Also, a target can make a Wisdom saving throw at the end of each of its turns. On a successful save, the spell ends for that creature. The spell also ends early on a creature if remove curse is cast on it.

Command

1st-level enchantment
Components
V
Duration
1 round
Casting Time
1 action
Range
60 feet
You speak a one-word command to a creature you can see within range. The target must succeed on a Wisdom saving throw or follow the command on its next turn. The spell has no effect if the target is undead, if it doesn't understand your language, or if your command is directly harmful to it.
Some typical commands and their effects follow. You might issue a command other than one described here. If you do so, the DM determines how the target behaves. If the target can't follow your command, the spell ends.
Approach. The target moves toward you by the shortest and most direct route, ending its turn if it moves within 5 feet of you.
Drop. The target drops whatever it is holding and then ends its turn.
Flee. The target spends its turn moving away from you by the fastest available means.
Grovel. The target falls prone and then ends its turn.
Halt. The target doesn't move and takes no actions. A flying creature stays aloft, provided that it is able to do so. If it must move to stay aloft, it flies the minimum distance needed to remain in the air.
At Higher Levels. When you cast this spell using a spell slot of 2nd level or higher, you can affect one additional creature for each slot level above 1st. The creatures must be within 30 feet of each other when you target them.
Available To
Bard, cleric, paladin
Source
PHB, TCE
