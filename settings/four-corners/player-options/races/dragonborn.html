Dragonborn

Dragonborn

  • Inhabitants of Dragon Island, along with Kobolds
  • Two group: metallic and chromatic
  • Each group reveres respective dragons, which are technically their ancestors
  • "Ancient Ones" were dragonborn who lived for centuries, and grew to huge sizes

Game Mechanics

Dragonborn

Abilities. Dragonborn have the following ability score tendencies.
Draconic Strength. Dragonborn generally have a high Strength score thanks to their heritage.
Draconic Senses. Some dragonborn have especially sharp senses, associated with a high Wisdom score.
Zealous Devotion. Many dragonborn dedicate their life to a cause, reflected by a high Charisma score. Usually this cause is related to the ideals of a dragon.
Alignment. On Dragon Island, dragonborn of both creeds follow the examples set by their draconic forefathers. Metallic dragonborn on the island are intensely righteous and even callous, but never malicious. On the other hand, the chromatic dragonborn are egotistical, greedy, and ruled by strength.
Away from Dragon Island, these ideological trends are significantly less pronounced. Whatever an individual dragonborn's outlook, it is almost surely in the extreme; the only alignment that is rare in dragonborn is true neutrality.
Age. Dragonborn spend 1 month in an egg before hatching. Youths reach maturity at age 12 when they are 4 to 5 feet tall, but continue to grow their entire lives. Their wings typically develop around age 25. At old ages, their still-growing size makes them increasingly vulnerable to heart failure. Your old age threshold is 70 years, with an aging rate of 1 year.
Languages. You are proficient in Draconic. Dragonborn are generally monolingual on Dragon Island, and most dragonborn who leave the island still retain their native tongue.
Size. Your size is Medium. Adult dragonborn usually reach 6 feet tall within 20 years, after which their growth starts to slow. Even so, elder dragonborn often exceed 8 feet in height. Dragonborn also grow heavier throughout their life, with adults ranging from 200 to 400 pounds on average as they age.
Speed. Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
Darkvision. Inheriting keen eyes from dragons, you have darkvision out to a range of 60 feet.
Powerful Build. ​You are unusually bulky. You count as one size larger than your actual size for the purpose of grappling and being grappled, wielding heavy weapons, determining your carrying capacity, and determining the weight you can push, drag, or lift.
Draconic Ancestry. Your draconic heritage manifests in several ways based on your draconic hue. Pick one dragon type from the Draconic Ancestry table. Your choice affects your Breath Weapon and Damage Resistance traits.

Breath Weapon. You can accumulate elemental energy within yourself and unleash it with your breath. You have a number of breath weapon dice equal to 2 + half your level, which are d6s. As a minor action, you can roll and expend any number of these dice and force each creature in your breath weapon area to make a saving throw. The shape, saving throw, and damage type are determined by your Draconic Ancestry. The DC is 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength or Constitution score (your choice). A creature takes damage equal the number rolled on the expended dice on a failed save, or half as much on a success.
You regain all expended breath weapon dice at the end of a short or long rest.
Damage Resistance. You have resistance to the damage type associated with your Draconic Ancestry.

Suggested Feats

Shielding Wings

Prerequisite: Dragonborn
Cost: 5 feat points
Your bulk makes flying difficult, but your tough wings make excellent shields. You gain the following benefits:
  • Your wings count as a kite shield, and you are proficient with them.
  • Your base AC can be calculated as 10 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength score, regardless of your armor.
  • If you also have the Shield Master feat, you can use the minor action to shove each creature in a 15-foot cube instead of just one creature if you do so with your wings.

Soaring Wings

Prerequisite: Dragonborn
Cost: 5 feat points
Your wings are strong enough for flight. You gain a fly speed of 40 feet. If you also have the Shielding Wings feat or are wearing heavy armor, this fly speed is halved.

Shield Master

Shield Master

Prerequisite: Proficiency with shields
Cost: 3 feat points
You have mastered advanced shield techniques. You gain the following benefits using a shield you're proficient with:
  • Donning or doffing a shield costs 1 less action point than normal.
  • You can use a make a special push attack using your shield. You are proficient with this attack, and it doesn't have the unarmed property. If you take the Attack action on your turn, you can make this attack as a minor action on the same turn.
  • If you're wielding a shield and are subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to take only half damage, you can use your reaction to take no damage if you succeed on the saving throw, interposing your shield between yourself and the source of the effect.

Prone

Prone

While prone, you experience the following effects:
  • The only movement mode available to you is crawling.
  • You have disadvantage on attack rolls.
  • An attack roll against you has advantage if the attacker is within 5 feet of you. Otherwise, the attack roll has disadvantage.
You can end this condition by standing up, which requires spending half of your walk speed when you move.
This condition is commonly caused by falling, falling unconscious, and the trip attack.

Falling

Falling

When you begin falling, you immediately fall 60 feet. While falling, you drop another 180 feet at the start of each of your turns.
On impact, you take one size die of bludgeoning damage for every 10 feet you fell, to a maximum of 20 dice. You land prone, unless you avoid taking damage from the fall.
You can use your reaction to mitigate the damage from landing. When you do so, make an Acrobatics check and reduce the fall damage by half the result.
If you land in a creature's space, that creature must make a DC 10 Dexterity saving throw. It can choose to fail. On a failed save, the fall damage is split evenly between you and it.
