Dragonborn

Dragonborn

  • Inhabitants of Dragon Island, along with Kobolds
  • Two group: metallic and chromatic
  • Each group reveres respective dragons, which are technically their ancestors
  • "Ancient Ones" were dragonborn who lived for centuries, and grew to huge sizes

Game Mechanics

Abilities

Draconic Strength. Dragonborn generally have a high Strength score thanks to their heritage.
Draconic Senses. Some dragonborn have especially sharp senses, associated with a high Wisdom score.
Zealous Devotion. Many dragonborn dedicate their life to a cause, reflected by a high Charisma score. Usually this cause is related to the ideals of a dragon.

Alignment

On Dragon Island, dragonborn of both creeds follow the examples set by their draconic forefathers. Metallic dragonborn on the island are intensely righteous and even callous, but never malicious. On the other hand, the chromatic dragonborn are egotistical, greedy, and ruled by strength.
Away from Dragon Island, these ideological trends are significantly less pronounced. Whatever an individual dragonborn's outlook, it is almost surely in the extreme; the only alignment that is rare in dragonborn is true neutrality.

Age

Dragonborn spend 1 month in an egg before hatching. Youths reach maturity at age 12 when they are 4 to 5 feet tall, but continue to grow their entire lives. Their wings typically develop around age 25. At old ages, their still-growing size makes them increasingly vulnerable to heart failure. Your old age threshold is 70 years, with an aging rate of 1 year.

Languages

Dragonborn are generally monolingual on Dragon Island, and most dragonborn who leave the island still retain their native tongue.

Size

Adult dragonborn usually reach 6 feet tall within 20 years, after which their growth starts to slow. Even so, elder dragonborn often exceed 8 feet in height. Dragonborn also grow heavier throughout their life, with adults ranging from 200 to 400 pounds on average as they age.

Breath Weapon. You can accumulate elemental energy within yourself and unleash it with your breath. You have a number of breath weapon dice equal to 2 + half your level, which are d6s. Shielding Wings

Prerequisite: Dragonborn
Cost: 4 feat points
Your bulk makes flying difficult, but your tough wings make excellent shields. You gain the following benefits:
  • Your wings count as a kite shield, and you are proficient with them.
  • Your base AC can be calculated as 10 + your proficiency bonus + your Strength score, regardless of your armor.
  • If you also have the Shield Master feat, you can use the minor action to shove each creature in a 15-foot cube instead of just one creature if you do so with your wings.

Soaring Wings

Prerequisite: Dragonborn
Cost: 4 feat points
Your wings are strong enough for flight. You gain a fly speed of 40 feet. If you also have the Shielding Wings feat or are wearing heavy armor, this fly speed is halved.

Jumping

Jumping

Jumping is a movement modifier that lets you clear gaps or obstacles. Instead of the usual movement cost, you must spend 1 foot of movement for every foot you jump vertically or horizontally (whichever is higher).
When you jump, perform the following series of calculations; don't round until the end. The resulting value is the maximum number of feet you can jump.
  1. Start with your passive Athletics. Alternatively, you can make an Athletics check and use the result instead.
  2. If you did not get a running start immediately before jumping, divide the value by 2. To get a running start, you must move in a straight line in the direction you want to jump for a number of feet determined by your size, as shown in the Running Start Distances table. The speed you use to get a running start must be the same speed you use to jump.
  3. To determine the vertical distance you can clear, divide the value by 2 again.
If you land in difficult terrain, you must succeed on a DC 10 Acrobatics check or fall prone.

Light

Light

There are three categories of illumination: bright light, dim light, and darkness.
A source of light typically emits bright light in a small radius and dim light in a larger radius; these distances are given in parentheses. For example, a fire that sheds light (20/40 ft.) produces bright light within 20 feet and dim light for another 20 feet beyond that.

Bright Light

Bright light lets most creatures see normally. Even gloomy days provide bright light, as do torches, lanterns, fires, and other sources of illumination within a specific radius.

Dim Light

Dim light, also called shadows, creates a lightly obscured area. An area of dim light is usually a boundary between a source of bright light, such as a torch, and surrounding darkness. The soft light of twilight and dawn also counts as dim light. A particularly brilliant full moon might bathe the land in dim light.

Darkness

Darkness creates a heavily obscured area. Characters face darkness outdoors at night (even most moonlit nights), within the confines of an unlit dungeon or a subterranean vault, or in an area of magical darkness.

Obscured

Obscured

When vision in an area is hindered by smoke, darkness, or a similar phenomenon, the area is obscured. There are two degrees of obscurity.

Lightly Obscured

In a lightly obscured area, such as dim light, patchy fog, or moderate foliage, creatures have disadvantage on Perception checks that rely on sight.

Heavily Obscured

A heavily obscured area, such as darkness, opaque fog, or dense foliage, blocks vision entirely. Nothing in that area can be seen.
