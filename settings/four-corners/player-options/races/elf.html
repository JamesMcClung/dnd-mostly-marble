Elf

Elf

Elves are native to the northern slopes of Verdania. They have long pointy ears and lean builds as a rule.
  • High elves: slightly taller, tan/copper skin, green-blue-white eyes, brown-gold hair
  • Wood elves: slightly shorter, light skin, green-yellow-brown eyes, brown-orange-red hair
  • Hair turns silver in old age (70+ years)
    • Only mortal elves and elves that were old before the Blessing have silver hair
  • Both of the largest subgroups, wood elves and high elves, tend to live in isolated communities.
  • Verdania is extremely poor in metals, so elves are generally inexperienced with metalwork. Instead, high elves make use of certain gemstones in the volcano. Peridot, spinel, and garnet are abundant, while pockets of quartz are scarce but rich. There is even well-hidden vein of kimberlite, named after the city built upon it.
  • Elves are unaging, and it is rare for one to die of violence or disease due to their wariness and medicinal expertise. This has significantly impacted Elvish culture. For instance, elves hold murder to be the worst crime, and children are uncommon. Every elf knows every other elf in his village or city, and it is common to know most elves in neighboring settlements as well. Most elves are masters of several disciplines, but those who have committed to one discipline are among the best in the world at it. Older elves spend more of their time meditating or practicing art.

Blessed Elves

Wood Elves (Omavir)

Drawn to druidic magic, wood elves meld their homes and villages into the large redwood-like trees near the coast.
  • Excellent hunters and gatherers, supplemented by magically-grown berries and wild vegetables.
  • Wood elves have no tamed animals, including horses. Instead, a wood elf that needs to move fast seeks out a wild giant elk to take them.

High Elves (Nauvir)

High elves use their affinity for the arcane arts to construct hidden towers and citadels in the mountain ridges.
  • Since they keep the locations of their cities secret, high elf individuals from different cities meet in predetermined places in the wild to trade. This trading is facilitated by the sending spell.
  • High elves subsist entirely on magically-accelerated agriculture within their cities. They occasionally hunt dangerous beasts for glory, but never for food.
  • Some high elves have tamed griffons and use them for travel.

Mortal Elves

Mortal Elves were separated from their cousins before the Blessing, and never received the gift of immortality.

Sea Elves (Maredrin)

Jungle and Sea Elves trace their ancestry to proto-Elven nobility. According to legend, Shaitan attempted to destroy a prosperous Elven kingdom by expelling so much lava from Verdoa that the land sunk into the sea. Their ruler King Mo'ire, attempting to save everyone, wished for his people to be saved. Only his house was affected, becoming the first Sea Elves. The rest perished, and the ruins of the city lie at the bottom of the Verdan Gulf buried under hundreds of feet of cooled lava.
Sea Elves, or Maredrin in their language, have generally remained in the Verdan Gulf, occasionally venturing out to trade with Triton. Maredrin despise Sahuagin and patrol the gulf to keep them—and other marine threats—out. They worship their ancestors, especially the ancient king who saved them. The Maredrin tend to be envious of the Blessing and are especially bitter about the relative safety that the Blessed Elves enjoy, which they claim is thanks to their efforts.

Jungle Elves (Maledrin)

Relatively recently, one faction of Maredrin was exiled and forced to live on land. These became the Jungle Elves, or Maledrin (also spelled Maladrin), who inhabit the mangrove swamps and tropical jungles of Verdania. Their crime was worshipping the kraken they named Lolugo. The kraken was slain long ago by a coalition of Triton and Maredrin, but the Maledrin continue to worship it as their true god.
The Maledrin believe that Lolugo can be brought back by blood sacrifices and aberrant magic in a practice called Aquorism. They worship aberrations as lesser gods. <redacted> In combat, the Maledrin take captives in order to ritually sacrifice them later. They occasionally ambush Maredrin patrols, but they are especially fond of hunting Merfolk. A Maledrin captain who fails to take enough captives must sacrifice members of his own family as punishment.

Fey Elves (Eladrin)

15,000 years ago, the Eladrin were a group of proto-Elves who were taken in by a new god, Pahi. According to legend his prophet, a nymph called Uhane, convinced them to sacrifice their souls to help create an entirely new world for them to rule. <redacted> This parallel reality, initially only a mirroring a small portion of Verdania, gradually grew to become the Feywild. The Eladrin no longer rule the Feywild alone, as it simply too big now and more creatures inhabit it.

Game Mechanics

Abilities

Natural Grace. Elves are known for their grace and agility. They usually have a high Dexterity score.
Ancient Wisdom. Thanks to their supernaturally long lives, Blessed elves almost always have a high Intelligence or Wisdom score.
Confident by Necessity. Maredrin and maledrin tend to have harsher lives, demanding resilience. The Feywild encourages a different kind of confidence in eladrin. For these reasons, mortal elves have a high Charisma score on average.

Alignment

The mortal elves have a diverse range of alignments, more so than humans. Sea elves are overall neutral, with more spread in law/chaos than in good/evil. The danger of their undersea home makes many sea elves callous to others' misfortune, and jealousy of the Blessing drives some towards evil. Jungle elves are outright evil as they seek to revive their god, a slain kraken, via blood sacrifices. On the other hand, fey elves are whimsical and lighthearted, nearly always chaotic and generally good.

Age

The mortal elves have comparable lives to humans, having an old age threshold of 70 years and an aging rate of 5 years.

Size

Your size is Medium. Elves are overall slenderer than humans. Maledrin are slightly shorter than humans, but the rest are about the same height.

Traits

Enhanced Darkvision. Your innate darkvision allows you to see in starlight as if it were bright light regardless of the actual light level, but distances beyond 60 feet are lightly obscured as if by mist. Instead of perceiving the usual color spectrum, most things under starlight take on shades of gray, but certain plants, animals, and minerals exhibit completely different colors that only elves can see.
Trance. You don't need to sleep. Instead, you meditate deeply, remaining semiconscious, for half as long as normally required (still minimum 1 hour). While meditating, you can dream after a fashion; such dreams are actually mental exercises that have become reflexive through years of practice.

Subraces

Select one of the subraces below.

High Elf (Nauvir)

Blessed. You do not grow old, and are immune to the effects of magical aging.
Languages. You are proficient in Elvish and one human language of your choice. Most Blessed elves know Sonnern, the language used for most arcane study and the basis for the modern Elvish alphabet. You can also read and write the Old Script, in which each glyph is topologically unique.
Cantrip. You know one cantrip of you choice from the wizard spell list. Intelligence is your spellcasting ability for it. You can change the cantrip granted by this feature by spending 8 hours over the course of 2 days practicing. You can practice during short rests and long rests, but not while trancing.

Wood Elf (Omavir)

Blessed. You do not grow old, and are immune to the effects of magical aging.
Languages. You are proficient in Elvish and one human language of your choice. Most Blessed elves know Sonnern, the language used for most arcane study and the basis for the modern Elvish alphabet. You can also read and write the Old Script, in which each glyph is topologically unique.
Fleet of Foot. Your base walking speed increases to 35 feet.
Mask of the Wild. You can attempt to Hide even when you are only lightly obscured by foliage, heavy rain, falling snow, mist, and other natural phenomena.
Friend of Beasts. Through sounds and gestures, you can convey simple ideas to Medium and Large beasts, and understand their emotional state. You have advantage on Animal Handling checks made on such beasts.
Home in the Trees. Your good balance and strong fingers are suited for climbing high in trees. You have advantage on Athletics made to climb trees or other plants. You are also able to rest comfortably in trees without risk of falling.

Sea Elf (Maredrin)

The King's Wish. You have a swim speed of 30 feet, and you can breathe air and water.
Languages. You are proficient in Marese and either Merrish or Tritonian (your choice). Marese is the native language of Maredrin, and many Maredrin have dealings with Merfolk or Tritons.
Underwater Camouflage. You have advantage on Stealth checks underwater.
Underwater Grace. When you take the Dodge action underwater, you also gain the benefits of the Dash and Disengage actions.

Jungle Elf (Maledrin)

The King's Wish. You have a swim speed of 30 feet, and you can breathe air and water.
Languages. You are proficient in Malese. Malese is the native language of the Maledrin and is closely related to Marese.
Jungle Walker. Your body is built for climbing quickly and surely. You have a climb speed of 20 feet and advantage on Acrobatics checks to avoid falling due to narrow walkspace. You also ignore difficult terrain due to dense plant growth.

Fey Elf (Eladrin)

Languages. You are proficient in Sylvan and High Sylvan. High Sylvan branched off from Elvish long before the Blessing and is spoken only by the aristocracy of the Feywild, including most Eladrin. Sylvan is the vulgar form of High Sylvan and is spoken by common denizens of the Feywild.
Fey Ancestry. Your creature type is Humanoid, but you also register as a Fey to abilities that detect fey. You have advantage on saving throws against being charmed.
Glamour. As a standard action, you can create an illusion to make yourself look different. The illusion lasts until you use a standard action to end it or to make a new illusion. The illusion doesn't affect your clothing or equipment and you can't change your height or body shape, but otherwise the extent of the illusion is up to you.
The illusion fails to hold up to physical inspection. For example, if you use this feature to add horns to your head, objects pass through the horns, and anyone who touches them would feel nothing or would feel your head and hair.
To discern that you are disguised, a creature can use the Search action to inspect your appearance and make an Intelligence (Investigation) check. The DC is equal to 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Charisma score.
Flare Glamour. Starting at 3rd level, you can flare your glamour for a more potent effect. When you use Glamour, you can augment your guise with any of the following otherworldly elements: an aura of light or darkness, up to a foot of increased height, altered clothing and equipment, indistinct sounds, or the amplification of your voice. You have advantage on Charisma checks for the duration of the Glamour.
You may also choose to evoke fear or awe. If you do so, each creature within 10 feet of you that can see and hear you must make a Wisdom saving throw against your Glamour DC or be frightened (fear) or charmed (awe) until they can no longer see nor hear your Glamour, or until they take damage.
When you use your Glamour in this way, it ends after 1 minute, and you can't use Glamour again until you finish a short or long rest.
Feybound. Your soul is tied to the Feywild. You age half as quickly in the Feywild and can sense a creature's karma, but you can't cast spells or use magic outside of it.

Ending the Bond

Some individuals have found ways of severing their tie and restoring their ability to use magic. One way (but not the only way) you can accomplish this is with the Severed Bonds feat.

Obscured

When vision in an area is hindered by smoke, darkness, or a similar phenomenon, the area is obscured. There are two degrees of obscurity.

Lightly Obscured

In a lightly obscured area, such as dim light, patchy fog, or moderate foliage, creatures have disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.

Heavily Obscured

Light

Light

The presence or absence of light in an environment creates three categories of illumination: bright light, dim light, and darkness.

Bright Light

Bright light lets most creatures see normally. Even gloomy days provide bright light, as do torches, lanterns, fires, and other sources of illumination within a specific radius.

Dim Light

Dim light, also called shadows, creates a lightly obscured area. An area of dim light is usually a boundary between a source of bright light, such as a torch, and surrounding darkness. The soft light of twilight and dawn also counts as dim light. A particularly brilliant full moon might bathe the land in dim light.

Darkness

Darkness creates a heavily obscured area. Characters face darkness outdoors at night (even most moonlit nights), within the confines of an unlit dungeon or a subterranean vault, or in an area of magical darkness.

Cover

Cover

Walls, trees, creatures, and other obstacles can provide cover during combat, making a target more difficult to harm. A target can benefit from cover only when an attack or other effect originates on the opposite side of the cover.
There are three degrees of cover. If a target is behind multiple sources of cover, only the most protective degree of cover applies; the degrees aren't added together. For example, if a target is behind a creature that gives half cover and a tree trunk that gives three-quarters cover, the target has three-quarters cover.

Half Cover

A target with half cover has a +2 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws. A target has half cover if an obstacle blocks at least half of its body. The obstacle might be a low wall, a large piece of furniture, a narrow tree trunk, or a creature, whether that creature is an enemy or a friend.

Three-Quarters Cover

A target with three-quarters cover has a +5 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws. A target has three-quarters cover if about three-quarters of it is covered by an obstacle. The obstacle might be a portcullis, an arrow slit, or a thick tree trunk.

Total Cover

A target with total cover can't be targeted directly by an attack or a spell, although some spells can reach such a target by including it in an area of effect. A target has total cover if it is completely concealed by an obstacle.

Jumping

Jumping

Jumping is a movement modifier that lets you clear gaps or obstacles. Instead of the usual movement cost, you must spend 1 foot of movement for every foot you jump vertically or horizontally (whichever is higher).
When you jump, perform the following series of calculations; don't round until the end. The resulting value is the maximum number of feet you can jump.
  1. Start with your passive Athletics. Alternatively, you can make an Athletics check and use the result instead.
  2. If you did not get a running start immediately before jumping, divide the value by 2 again. To get a running start, you must move in a straight line in the direction you want to jump for a number of feet determined by your size, as shown in the Running Start Distances table. The speed you use to get a running start must be the same speed you use to jump.
  3. To determine the vertical distance you can clear, divide the value by 2 again.
If you land in difficult terrain, you must succeed on a DC 10 Acrobatics check or fall prone.

Falling

Falling

When you begin falling, you immediately fall 60 feet. While falling, you drop another 180 feet at the start of each of your turns.
On impact, you take one size die of bludgeoning damage for every 10 feet you fell, to a maximum of 20 dice. You land prone, unless you avoid taking damage from the fall.
You can use your reaction to mitigate the damage from landing. When you do so, make an Acrobatics check and reduce the fall damage by half the result.
If you land in a creature's space, that creature must make a DC 10 Dexterity saving throw. It can choose to fail. On a failed save, the fall damage is split evenly between you and it.

Thrown

Thrown

If a weapon has the "thrown" property, you can make a ranged attack with it by throwing it. If the weapon is a melee weapon, you use the same ability for the attack roll and damage roll that you would use for a melee attack with the weapon.
The numbers shown in parentheses after this property's listing are the weapon's normal range and long range when thrown, in that order.
For example, if you throw a handaxe, you use your Strength, but if you throw a dagger, you can use either your Strength or your Dexterity, since the dagger has the finesse property.
