Tiefling

Tiefling

A tiefling, which translates roughly to "low one" in Sonnern, is a cursed being. A child of any race may be born a tiefling, and virtually any parent would abandon such a child as soon as the curse became evident. Tieflings are shunned by most people due to prejudices and fears—which may be justified—concerning them. It is well-known that tiefling behave erratically, and many people believe that their supernatural visage and powers mark them as evil.

The Curse

The Tiefling's Curse usually manifests as oddly colored skin, hair, and eyes, with the eyes being completely monochromatic. These traits may not appear until the tiefling child is a year old. In extreme cases, a tiefling can have horns, sharp teeth, claws instead of nails, slits for a nose, or even a tail; these traits appear much sooner, even before birth.
The true impact of the Curse is mental, not physical. A tiefling gets impulses to act either chaotically or orderly, and feels discomfort from resisting these impulses. Some tieflings even hear voices encouraging such behaviors. The Curse strengthens with age, both mentally and physically. When a tiefling fully succumbs to the Curse, they act solely on these impulses for few years before entering a coma and ultimately dying. The corrupted body feat represents the exceptional tiefling whose Curse-effected deformities are beneficial.
Magic can suspend the mental impacts of the Curse, but nothing truly cure a tiefling.

Lineage Prerequisites

You can't take levels in this lineage until you reach certain levels as shown in the Tiefling table. For example, you can take your first level in the wizard class and your second level in the tiefling lineage, but you can't take another tiefling level until after you take at least two other class levels.
Additionally, before you can take any levels in the tiefling lineage, you must have the Corrupted Soul feat, reproduced below.

Corrupted Soul

Prerequisite: Special
Cost: 1 feat point
Special: you can only take this feat at level 1 when you create your character.
You are a tiefling: your soul is tainted by evil forces, cursing you with deformity and madness—but also granting certain powers. You register as a Fiend to abilities that detect fiends, your old age threshold and aging rate are reduced by a third, and your eyes are monochromatic globes. You may also have red skin, horns, a tail, hooves, or other physical characteristics that are abnormal for your race.
As a tiefling, you likely feel powerful compulsions or hear voices in your head encouraging you to act with extreme orderliness or chaos. Pick lawful or chaotic. You don't have to be that alignment, but your choice determines what kind of behavior your madness drives you towards.

Lineage Features

Hit Points

Hit Dice: 1d8 per tiefling level
Hit Points: 5 + your Constitution score per tiefling level

Tiefling Spellcasting

1st-level Tiefling feature
As a tiefling, you can draw on the arcane energy of your affliction to cast spells.
Cantrips. You learn the thaumaturgy cantrip plus one other Arcane cantrip of your choice.
Casting Spells of 1st Level and Higher. Choose one 1st-level Arcane spell. You can cast that spell once with this feature, regaining the ability to do so when you finish a short rest.
You gain the ability to cast more spells at higher levels. Whenever you gain an tiefling level, choose a Divine spell of each level shown in the Short Rest Spells and Long Rest Spells columns of the Tiefling table. You can cast each spell once, regaining the ability to do so when you finish a short rest (if the spell was from the Short Rest Spells column) or a long rest (if the spell was from the Long Rest Spells column).
Spellcasting Ability. When you gain this lineage, choose Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma as your spellcasting ability. You use the chosen ability whenever an tiefling spell or feature refers to your spellcasting ability. In addition, you use the chosen ability score when setting the saving throw DC for an tiefling spell you cast and when making an attack roll with one.
Spell save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your spellcasting ability score
Spell attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your spellcasting ability score
Spellcasting Focus. You are your own spellcasting focus for your tiefling spells.

Darkvision

1st-level Tiefling feature
Thanks to your enhanced senses, you have darkvision out to a range of 60 feet. If you already have darkvision from your race, the range of that darkvision increases by 60 feet.

Maddening Corruption

1st-level Tiefling feature
You gain powers depending on your madness. If you are inclined towards order, you are a leguleian tiefling. If you inclined towards chaos, you are an atrophic tiefling. You gain additional powers when you reach higher levels as a tiefling, as shown in the Tiefling table.
Leguleian
Source: 4C
A leguleian tiefling is discomforted by disobedience or lapses in hierarchy. Many succomb to their compulsions, enforcing their wills with severe punishments. Leguleian nobles and warlords are among the most feared of their kinds.

Leguleian Resistance

1st-level Leguleian feature
You have resistance to fire damage.

Leguleian Madness

1st-level Leguleian feature
When you cast a tiefling spell, make a Charisma saving throw. The DC equals 10 + the level of the spell, and you can choose to fail. On a failure, you temporarily succomb to madness. The madness lasts until the end of your next turn if the spell was a cantrip; for 1 minute if the spell was between 1st and 3rd level; for 1 hour if the spell was between 4th and 6th level; or for 8 hours if the spell was between 7th and 9th level. This madness counts as a curse, and can thus be removed by spells such as remove curse.
While the madness persists, your alignment becomes Lawful in the extreme if it wasn't already, and you have advantage on Charisma saving throws.

Order of Command

2nd-level Leguleian feature
You can cast command at will, but you take 3 psychic damage when you do so unless you were already afflicted by the madness of your Leguleian Madness. This damage can't otherwise be reduced or prevented.
If a target succeeds on its saving throw against the spell, it can choose to automatically succeed against further castings of command with this feature for 1 hour.

Order of Punishment

3rd-level Leguleian feature
When you cast command using your Order of Command and the target succeeds on its saving throw or is unaffected by the spell, you can cause the target to take 2d10 fire damage.

Order of Tyranny

4th-level Leguleian feature
When you cast command using your Order of Command, you can target a number of creatures up to your spellcasting ability score (minimum 2).

Despotic Delerium

5th-level Leguleian feature
While afflicted by the madness of your Leguleian Madness, you gain the following benefits:
  • Damage can't cause you to lose concentration on a spell you cast using a tiefling feature.
  • Targets have disadvantage on their saving throws against command when you cast it with your Order of Command.
  • The fire damage of your Order of Punishment increases to 5d10.
Atrophic
Source: 4C
An atrophic tiefling is an unwitting agent of chaos. Most fight their madness as long as they can due to the ruinous effects it can have on their lives, and those who succomb often become dangerous criminals.

Atrophic Madness

1st-level Atrophic feature
When you use a tiefling feature to cast a spell, make a Charisma saving throw. The DC equals 10 + the level of the spell, and you can choose to fail. On a failure, you temporarily succomb to madness. The madness lasts until the end of your next turn if the spell was a cantrip; for 1 minute if the spell was between 1st and 3rd level; for 1 hour if the spell was between 4th and 6th level; or for 8 hours if the spell was between 7th and 9th level. This madness counts as a curse, and can be removed by spells such as remove curse.
While the madness persists, your alignment becomes Chaotic in the extreme if it wasn't already, and you have advantage on Charisma saving throws.

Atrophic Resistance

1st-level Atrophic feature
You have resistance to necrotic damage.

Incite Destruction

2nd-level Atrophic feature
You can cast inflict wounds at will, but you take 3 psychic damage when you do so unless you were already afflicted by the madness of your Atrophic Madness or willingly fail the saving throw against it. This damage can't otherwise be reduced or prevented.

Incite Decay

3rd-level Atrophic feature
When you hit a target with inflict wounds cast using your Incite Destruction, you can cause the target to take ongoing 3d10 necrotic damage (Constitution save ends). If the target was already taking this ongoing damage, the existing ongoing damage increases by 1d10.

Incite Betrayal

4th-level Atrophic feature
When you cast inflict wounds using your Incite Destruction, you can use a different creature to deliver the spell as if you had cast it. The creature must be within 60 feet of you, you must be able to see it, and it must be taking ongoing damage from your Incite Decay feature. It doesn't need to be willing. You choose the target (which can be the creature itself) and use your own spell attack score.

Anarchic Delerium

5th-level Atrophic feature
While afflicted by the madness of your Atrophic Madness, you gain the following benefits:
  • Damage can't cause you to lose concentration on a tiefling spell.
  • When you use Incite Destruction to cast inflict wounds, you can target two different creatures instead of one. You can choose to deliver the spell via Incite Betrayal separately for each target.

Sudden Omen

3rd-level Tiefling feature
You can cast thaumaturgy once on each of your turns as a free action and without components.

Suggested Feats

Corrupted Body

Prerequisite: Tiefling lineage
Cost: 4 feat points
Your affliction warps your body beyond what is normally survivable, making you stronger for it. Choose one of the following benefits:
  1. You grow bat-like wings, granting you a fly speed of 30 feet. This fly speed is halved while you wear heavy armor.
  2. Your skin hardens to chitinous or osseous plates. Your base AC can be calculated as 10 + your proficiency bonus + your Constitution score, regardless of your armor.
  3. Your tail, horns, and/or claws are empowered by your curse. Your claws have the unarmed, finesse and light and have a damage die of 2d4 slashing; your tail has the unarmed, finesse, light, and reach 10 properties and has a damage die of 1d4 bludgeoning; and/or your horns have the unarmed property and have a damage die of 1d12 piercing. On a hit, your unarmed attacks deal an extra 1d6 fire damage (if you are a leguleian tiefling) or necrotic damage (if you are an atrophic tiefling).
You can take this feat multiple times. Each time you do, choose a different benefit.

Obscured

Obscured

When vision in an area is hindered by smoke, darkness, or a similar phenomenon, the area is obscured. There are two degrees of obscurity.

Lightly Obscured

In a lightly obscured area, such as dim light, patchy fog, or moderate foliage, creatures have disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.

Heavily Obscured

A heavily obscured area, such as darkness, opaque fog, or dense foliage, blocks vision entirely. Nothing in that area can be seen.

Light

Light

The presence or absence of light in an environment creates three categories of illumination: bright light, dim light, and darkness.

Bright Light

Bright light lets most creatures see normally. Even gloomy days provide bright light, as do torches, lanterns, fires, and other sources of illumination within a specific radius.

Dim Light

Dim light, also called shadows, creates a lightly obscured area. An area of dim light is usually a boundary between a source of bright light, such as a torch, and surrounding darkness. The soft light of twilight and dawn also counts as dim light. A particularly brilliant full moon might bathe the land in dim light.

Darkness

Darkness creates a heavily obscured area. Characters face darkness outdoors at night (even most moonlit nights), within the confines of an unlit dungeon or a subterranean vault, or in an area of magical darkness.

Prone

While prone, you experience the following effects:
  • The only movement mode available to you is crawling.
  • You have disadvantage on attack rolls.
  • An attack roll against you has advantage if the attacker is within 5 feet of you. Otherwise, the attack roll has disadvantage.
You can end this condition by standing up, which requires spending half of your walk speed when you move.
This condition is commonly caused by falling, falling unconscious, and the trip attack.

Falling

Falling

When you begin falling, you immediately fall 60 feet. While falling, you drop another 180 feet at the start of each of your turns.
On impact, you take one size die of bludgeoning damage for every 10 feet you fell, to a maximum of 20 dice. You land prone, unless you avoid taking damage from the fall.
You can use your reaction to mitigate the damage from landing. When you do so, make an Acrobatics check and reduce the fall damage by half the result.
If you land in a creature's space, that creature must make a DC 10 Dexterity saving throw. It can choose to fail. On a failed save, the fall damage is split evenly between you and it.
