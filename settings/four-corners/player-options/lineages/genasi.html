Genasi

Genasi

Genasi are individuals infused with an element: air, earth, fire, or water. A genasi can be born to parents of any race.

Fall of Nature

Legends tell of genasi who could rival a Primordial with their elemental power. Such legends may have been exaggerated, but genasi power is certainly in decline. In living memory, no genasi has been more innately powerful than a typical elemental. It is postulated that the binding of the Primordials is somehow the cause of the decline.

Lineage Prerequisites

You can't take levels in this lineage until you reach certain levels as shown in the Genasi table. For example, you can take your first level in the wizard class and your second level in the genasi lineage, but you can't take another genasi level until after you take at least two other class levels.
Additionally, before you can take any levels in the genasi lineage, you must have the Elemental Soul feat, reproduced below.

Elemental Soul

Prerequisite: Special
Cost: 1 feat point
Special: you can only take this feat at level 1 when you create your character.
You are a genasi: your soul is imbued with primordial energy. Choose air, earth, fire, or water. You register as an Elemental to abilities that detect elementals, and your body is physically altered to some degree by the chosen element. A fire genasi might have red eyes or flaming hair, while an earth genasi might have stone-like skin.

Elemental Nature

1st-level Genasi feature
You learn to harness the powers of your element. You gain additional powers when you reach higher levels as a genasi, as shown in the Genasi table.
Air
Source: 4C
Air genasi are lighter than normal and typically have gray-tinged skin and hair. Powerful air genasi seem to fade into the air due to the translucence of their arms and legs.

Thunder Resistance

1st-level Air feature
You have resistance to thunder damage.

Unending Breath

1st-level Air feature
You can hold your breath indefinitely while you're not incapacitated.

Aerial Grace

1st-level Air feature
As a minor action, you can begin to focus on the air around you and use it to guide your movement. While you are in air, unencumbered, and maintain concentration, you gain the following benefits:
  • You weigh half as much as normal.
  • When you begin falling, you don't fall immediately and only fall 60 feet at the start of each of your turns. While falling, you can descend 1 foot and travel 2 feet horizontally by spending 1 foot of your walking speed's movement. When you land, you take no falling damage and can land on your feet.
  • You have advantage on Acrobatics checks.

Jet Boost

2nd-level Air feature
As a minor action, you can propel yourself in a straight line in any direction a number of feet equal to 5 times your spellcasting ability modifier. This motion does not provoke opportunity attacks.

Aerial Agility

3rd-level Air feature
While concentrating on your Aerial Grace, you have a hover speed of 60 feet.

Whirlwind Dodge

4th-level Air feature
Immediately before you would be hit by an attack, you can activate your Jet Boost as a reaction. Doing so causes a ranged attack to miss if you moved of its path, or a melee attack to miss if you move out of the attacker's reach. You can use this reaction multiple times per round, but you can't use it to move more than 5 times your spellcasting ability modifier in a single round.

Aerial Eminence

5th-level Air feature
Damage can no longer cause you to lose concentration on Aerial Grace. Additionally, while concentrating on Aerial Grace, you can create one of the following effects when you activate Jet Boost and move at least 5 feet:
  • Each creature within 10 feet of your starting position other than yourself must succeed on a Strength saving throw or be pushed up to 10 feet away.
  • Each creature within 10 feet of your starting position other than yourself must succeed on a Constitution saving throw or take 4d6 thunder damage, or half as much on a success.
Earth
Source: 4C
Earth genasi are heavier than normal and typically have brown-tinged skin and hair. Powerful earth genasi seem to be made of rock due to the toughen, earthy texture of their skin.

Lightning Resistance

1st-level Earth feature
You have resistance to lightning damage.

Stonewalker

1st-level Earth feature
You ignore difficult terrain made of earth or stone.

Earthen Strength

1st-level Earth feature
As a minor action, you can begin to focus on the earth beneath you and tap into its strength and stability. While you are on the ground and maintain concentration, you gain the following benefits:
  • At the start of each of your turns, you gain temporary hit points equal to your spellcasting ability modifier. They vanish when Earthen Strength ends.
  • You have tremorsense with a range of 15 feet.
  • You have advantage on Athletics checks.

Defensive Pillars

2nd-level Earth feature
You can cast earthen barrier on yourself at will, requiring no components. Doing so takes a great amount of sudden focus; if you are concentrating when you cast the spell in this way, you lose concentration.

Earthen Armor

3rd-level Earth feature
You use the earth to fortify yourself. While concentrating on your Earthen Strength, you gain a bonus to AC equal to the number of temporary hit points you currently have from it.

Stone Bunker

4th-level Earth feature
When you use Defensive Pillars to cast earthen barrier, the cover it grants is total cover instead of half cover.

Earthen Juggernaut

5th-level Earth feature
Damage can no longer cause you to lose concentration on Earthen Strength. Additionally, while concentrating on Earthen Strength, you gain the following benefits:
  • When you activate Earthen Strength, you can increase your size by 1 category for the duration.
  • Any damage you take other than psychic damage is reduced by an amount equal to your spellcasting ability modifier. This reduction occurs before applying resistances or vulnerabilities.
Fire
Source: 4C
Fire genasi typically have an aggressive disposition and red-tinged skin and hair. Powerful fire genasi resemble living candles due to flames replacing their hair.

Fire Resistance

1st-level Fire feature
You have resistance to fire damage.

Flameburst

1st-level Fire feature
You gain a melee spell attack and a ranged spell attack that you can make by taking the Attack action. The melee attack has a reach of 10 feet and a damage die of 1d12 fire. The ranged attack has a range of 60 feet and a damage die of 1d6 fire. You are proficient with both, and you use your spellcasting ability for their attack and damage rolls.

Fiery Brilliance

1st-level Fire feature
As a minor action, you can let fire consume and invigorate you. While you maintain concentration, you gain the following benefits:
  • Wisps of fire coil off you, shedding bright light out to a radius of 10 feet and dim light an additional 10 feet.
  • An aura of heat surrounds you out to a radius of 5 feet. When a creature other than you ends its turn within the aura, you cause it to take fire damage equal to your spellcasting ability modifier.
  • You have advantage on Intimidation checks.

Disperse Heat

2nd-level Fire feature
When you would take fire damage, you can use your reaction to become immune to the triggering fire damage. Doing so takes a great amount of sudden focus; if you are concentrating when you use this reaction, you lose concentration.

Flame Barrage

2nd-level Fire feature
When you take the Attack action on your turn, you can make two attacks with your Flameburst instead of one.

Fiery Wrath

3rd-level Fire feature
While concentrating on Fiery Brilliance, the damage dice of your melee and ranged Flameburst attacks are 2d12 and 2d6, respectively.

Redirect Heat

4th-level Fire feature
You are immune to fire damage. Disperse Heat now lets you make an attack with Flameburst as a reaction in response to being exposed to fire damage. You lose concentration after making this attack.

Fiery Nova

5th-level Fire feature
Damage can no longer cause you to lose concentration on Fiery Brilliance. Once on each of your turns while concentrating on Fiery Brilliance, when you hit a target with a Flameburst attack, you can create an explosion of fire centered at the target. The target and each creature within 10 feet of it other than yourself must make a Dexterity saving throw against your spell save DC. On a failed save, a creature takes 4d6 fire damage, or half as much on a success. You can then teleport to an unoccupied space you can see within the explosion.
Water
Source: 4C
Water genasi typically have a calm disposition and blue-tinged skin and hair. Powerful water genasi seem to be made of water due to water replacing their arms and legs.

Acid Resistance

1st-level Water feature
You have resistance to acid damage.

Waterborn

1st-level Water feature
You can breathe water, and you have a swim speed of 30 feet.

Aquatic Serenity

1st-level Water feature
As a minor action, you can begin attuning yourself to the water within and around you. While you maintain concentration, you gain the following benefits:
  • Your swim speed increases to 60 feet.
  • You have blindsight with a range of 15 feet, or 30 feet within water.
  • You have advantage on Insight checks.

Watery Discorporation

2nd-level Water feature
When you would take damage, you can use your reaction to briefly discorporate into a mass of water. You move up to 10 feet, or up to 20 feet within water. This movement doesn't provoke opportunity attacks, ignores difficult terrain, and lets you squeeze through a gap of any size unhindered. Additionally, any damage you take while moving in this way, including damage from the triggering attack, is halved. Discorporating takes a great amount of sudden focus; if you are concentrating when you use this reaction, you lose concentration.

Aquatic Sanctuary

3rd-level Water feature
While concentrating on Aquatic Serenity, you are immune to the charmed, frightened, and taunted conditions, and you can use the Treat Wounds option of the Heal action as a standard action.

Cleansing Discorporation

4th-level Water feature
You can use Watery Discorporation as a minor action, requiring no triggering attack and without ending your concentration. Additionally, when you use Watery Discorporation, you can end one effect causing you to be blinded, deafened, or poisoned.

Aquatic Rejuvenation

5th-level Water feature
Damage can no longer cause you to lose concentration on Aquatic Serenity. Additionally, while concentrating on Aquatic Serenity and bloodied, you regain a number of hit points equal to your spellcasting ability modifier at the start of each of your turns.

Difficult Terrain

Difficult Terrain

Every foot of movement in difficult terrain costs 1 extra foot. This rule is true even if multiple things in a space count as difficult terrain.
Low furniture, rubble, undergrowth, steep stairs, snow, and shallow bogs are examples of difficult terrain. The space of another creature, whether hostile or not, also counts as difficult terrain.

Concentration

Concentration

Some spells and other effects require you to maintain concentration in order to keep them active. If you lose concentration, such an effect ends. You can also choose to end concentration on any effect at any time (no action required).
If a spell must be maintained with concentration, that fact appears in its Duration entry, and the spell specifies how long you can concentrate on it.
Normal activity, such as moving and attacking, doesn't interfere with concentration. The following factors can break concentration:
  • You lose concentration if you invoke another effect that requires concentration. You can't concentrate on two effects at once.
  • Whenever you take damage while you are concentrating, you must make a Constitution saving throw to maintain your concentration. The DC equals 10 or half the damage you take, whichever number is higher. If you take damage from multiple sources, such as an arrow and a dragon's breath, you make a separate saving throw for each source of damage.
  • You lose concentration on an effect if you are incapacitated or if you die.
  • Certain environmental phenomena, such as a wave crashing over you while you're on a storm-tossed ship, may require you to succeed on a DC 10 Constitution saving throw to maintain concentration. This is at your DM's discretion.

Prone

Prone

While prone, you experience the following effects:
  • The only movement mode available to you is crawling.
  • You have disadvantage on attack rolls.
  • An attack roll against you has advantage if the attacker is within 5 feet of you. Otherwise, the attack roll has disadvantage.
You can end this condition by standing up, which requires spending half of your walk speed when you move.
This condition is commonly caused by falling, falling unconscious, and the trip attack.

Falling

Falling

When you begin falling, you immediately fall 60 feet. While falling, you drop another 180 feet at the start of each of your turns.
On impact, you take one size die of bludgeoning damage for every 10 feet you fell, to a maximum of 20 dice. You land prone, unless you avoid taking damage from the fall.
You can use your reaction to mitigate the damage from landing. When you do so, make an Acrobatics check and reduce the fall damage by half the result.
If you land in a creature's space, that creature must make a DC 10 Dexterity saving throw. It can choose to fail. On a failed save, the fall damage is split evenly between you and it.

Tremorsense

Cover

Cover

Walls, trees, creatures, and other obstacles can provide cover during combat, making a target more difficult to harm. A target can benefit from cover only when an attack or other effect originates on the opposite side of the cover.
There are three degrees of cover. If a target is behind multiple sources of cover, only the most protective degree of cover applies; the degrees aren't added together. For example, if a target is behind a creature that gives half cover and a tree trunk that gives three-quarters cover, the target has three-quarters cover.

Half Cover

A target with half cover has a +2 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws. A target has half cover if an obstacle blocks at least half of its body. The obstacle might be a low wall, a large piece of furniture, a narrow tree trunk, or a creature, whether that creature is an enemy or a friend.

Three-Quarters Cover

A target with three-quarters cover has a +5 bonus to AC and Dexterity saving throws. A target has three-quarters cover if about three-quarters of it is covered by an obstacle. The obstacle might be a portcullis, an arrow slit, or a thick tree trunk.

Total Cover

A target with total cover can't be targeted directly by an attack or a spell, although some spells can reach such a target by including it in an area of effect. A target has total cover if it is completely concealed by an obstacle.

Obscured

Obscured

When vision in an area is hindered by smoke, darkness, or a similar phenomenon, the area is obscured. There are two degrees of obscurity.

Lightly Obscured

In a lightly obscured area, such as dim light, patchy fog, or moderate foliage, creatures have disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.

Heavily Obscured

A heavily obscured area, such as darkness, opaque fog, or dense foliage, blocks vision entirely. Nothing in that area can be seen.

Light

Light

The presence or absence of light in an environment creates three categories of illumination: bright light, dim light, and darkness.

Bright Light

Bright light lets most creatures see normally. Even gloomy days provide bright light, as do torches, lanterns, fires, and other sources of illumination within a specific radius.

Dim Light

Dim light, also called shadows, creates a lightly obscured area. An area of dim light is usually a boundary between a source of bright light, such as a torch, and surrounding darkness. The soft light of twilight and dawn also counts as dim light. A particularly brilliant full moon might bathe the land in dim light.

Darkness

Darkness creates a heavily obscured area. Characters face darkness outdoors at night (even most moonlit nights), within the confines of an unlit dungeon or a subterranean vault, or in an area of magical darkness.

Teleporting

Teleporting

Teleporting lets you instantaneously travel from one location to another without going through the intervening space. You don't provoke opportunity attacks when you teleport.
When you teleport, all the equipment you are wearing and carrying teleports with you. If you are touching another creature when you teleport, that creature doesn't teleport with you unless otherwise specified.
If the destination space of your teleportation is occupied by another creature or blocked by a solid obstacle, you instead appear in the nearest unoccupied space of your choice.
Unless otherwise specified, you must see your destination when you teleport.

Jumping

Jumping

Jumping is a movement modifier that lets you clear gaps or obstacles. Instead of the usual movement cost, you must spend 1 foot of movement for every foot you jump vertically or horizontally (whichever is higher).
When you jump, perform the following series of calculations; don't round until the end. The resulting value is the maximum number of feet you can jump.
  1. Start with your passive Athletics. Alternatively, you can make an Athletics check and use the result instead.
  2. If you did not get a running start immediately before jumping, divide the value by 2 again. To get a running start, you must move in a straight line in the direction you want to jump for a number of feet determined by your size, as shown in the Running Start Distances table. The speed you use to get a running start must be the same speed you use to jump.
  3. To determine the vertical distance you can clear, divide the value by 2 again.
If you land in difficult terrain, you must succeed on a DC 10 Acrobatics check or fall prone.

Heal

Heal

Cost: varies (repeatable)
There are two uses of the Heal action.
Stabilize (2 AP). You attempt to quickly stabilize one dying creature within your reach. Make a DC 10 Medicine check. On a success, the target is stabilized.
Treat Wounds (10 Minutes). You tend to the injuries of a creature within your reach, spending at least 2 AP each turn while doing so. Make a DC 10 Medicine check at the end. On a success, the target regains a number of hit points equal to 1d6 + the target's maximum number of hit dice. Whatever the outcome, the target can't regain hit points in this way again until it finishes a short rest.
