Aasimar

Aasimar

Aasimar can be born to any race. Barring magic (an aasimar's celestial nature can be detected even while it is still in the womb), there is no sure way to distiniguish an aasimar child from a normal child, though all aasimar are exceptionially beautiful and hale. Most aasimar first manifest their powers during adolescence.

Portentous Birth

An aasimar's birth is often marked by a good or ill omen, depending on whether the aasimar is theurgic or malefic. See the Aasimar Omens table for examples of omens. It is said that the magnitude of the omen is commensurate to the impact of the aasimar's life on the world.

Innate Alignment

Unlike other races, aasimar never deviate from their expected alignment: good for theurgic, or evil for malefic. Even tieflings can fight their impulses towards chaos or orderliness, but for aasimar, there is nothing to fight.

Lineage Prerequisites

You can't take levels in this lineage until you reach certain levels as shown in the Aasimar table. For example, you can take your first level in the wizard class and your second level in the aasimar lineage, but you can't take another aasimar level until after you take at least two other class levels.
Additionally, before you can take any levels in the aasimar lineage, you must have the Celestial Soul feat, reproduced below.

Celestial Soul

Prerequisite: Special
Cost: 1 feat point
Special: you can only take this feat at level 1 when you create your character.
You are an aasimar: your soul was touched by a divine entity, blessing you with supernatural health, beauty, and even abilities. You register as a Celestial to abilities that detect celestials, and your old age threshold and aging rate are doubled.
As an aasimar, you were born good or evil depending on the divinity influencing your soul. You don't feel compulsions or hear a voice in your head; you simply are what you are. Pick any good or evil alignment as your alignment. It can't change by mundane means.

Lineage Features

Hit Points

Hit Dice: 1d8 per aasimar level
Hit Points: 5 + your Constitution score per aasimar level

Aasimar Spellcasting

1st-level Aasimar feature
As an aasimar, you possess a spark of divinity that you can draw on to cast spells.
Cantrips. You learn the thaumaturgy cantrip plus one other Divine cantrip of your choice.
Casting Spells of 1st Level and Higher. Choose one 1st-level Divine spell. You can cast that spell once with this feature, regaining the ability to do so when you finish a short rest.
You gain the ability to cast more spells at higher levels. Whenever you gain an aasimar level, choose a Divine spell of each level shown in the Short Rest Spells and Long Rest Spells columns of the Aasimar table. You can cast each spell once, regaining the ability to do so when you finish a short rest (if the spell was from the Short Rest Spells column) or a long rest (if the spell was from the Long Rest Spells column).
Spellcasting Ability. When you gain this lineage, choose Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma as your spellcasting ability. You use the chosen ability whenever an aasimar spell or feature refers to your spellcasting ability. In addition, you use the chosen ability score when setting the saving throw DC for an aasimar spell you cast and when making an attack roll with one.
Spell save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your spellcasting ability score
Spell attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your spellcasting ability score
Spellcasting Focus. You are your own spellcasting focus for your aasimar spells.

Darkvision

1st-level Aasimar feature
Thanks to your enhanced senses, you have darkvision out to a range of 60 feet. If you already have darkvision from your race, the range of that darkvision increases by 60 feet.

Celestial Conscience

1st-level Aasimar feature
You gain powers depending on your alignment. If you are good, you are a theurgic aasimar. If you are evil, you are a malefic aasimar. You gain additional powers when you reach higher levels as an aasimar, as shown in the Aasimar table.
Theurgic
Source: 4C
A theurgic aasimar is an archon of goodness and can't abide evil.

Theurgic Halo

1st-level Theurgic feature
As a minor Magic action, you can surround yourself in a luminous halo. The halo requires concentration to maintain, but otherwise lasts indefinitely.
The halo sheds magical bright light out to a range of 15 feet and dim light for another 15 feet. This light counts as having been created by a spell with a level equal to your aasimar level, and dispels magical darkness created by spells of that level or lower.
Your non-evil allies automatically succeed on death saving throws while in the bright light.

Protective Light

2nd-level Theurgic feature
While in the bright light of your Theurgic Halo, you and your non-evil allies have advantage on Wisdom saving throws.

Healing Light

3rd-level Theurgic feature
When you or a non-evil creature ends its turn in the bright light of your Theurgic Halo, you can cause it to regain a number of hit points equal to your spellcasting ability score (minimum 1).
You can use this feature a number of times equal to your spellcasting ability score (minimum of once). You regain all expended uses when you finish a long rest.

Purging Light

3rd-level Theurgic feature
When a creature ends its turn in the bright light of your Theurgic Halo, you can cause it to take 1d8 radiant damage, or 2d8 radiant damage if it is evil-aligned.

Heavenly Wings

4th-level Theurgic feature
You can manifest spectral wings as a minor action. The wings are translucent and glow softly, but otherwise you choose their appearance when you gain this feature. While the wings are active, you shed dim light in your space and have a fly speed of 40 feet. The wings remain until you use another minor action to dismiss them or until you are reduced to 0 hit points.

Consuming Radiance

5th-level Theurgic feature
When you manifest your Heavenly Wings, you can empower them. If you do so, your Theurgic Halo is also activated and remains active with the wings, no concentration required, and the distance of the bright and dim light is doubled. However, you take 5 radiant damage at the start of each of your turns as long as the wings are active. This damage can't be reduced or prevented.
Malefic
Source: 4C
A malefic aasimar is evil to the core and enjoys the suffering of others.

Malefic Nimbus

1st-level Malefic feature
As a minor Magic action, you can surround yourself in a shadowy nimbus. The nimbus requires concentration to maintain, but otherwise lasts indefinitely.
The nimbus creates an area of darkness within 10 feet of you. A creature other than you with darkvision can't see through this darkness, and nonmagical light can't illuminate it. This darkness counts as having been created by a spell with a level equal to your aasimar level, and dispels magical light created by spells of that level or lower.
While in the darkness, a dying creature automatically fails death saving throws and can't regain hit points.

Insidious Shadows

2nd-level Malefic feature
Creatures other than yourself have disadvantage on Wisdom saving throws while in the darkness of your Malefic Nimbus.

Scornful Shadows

3rd-level Malefic feature
When a creature other than yourself regains hit points or gains temporary hit points while within the darkness of your Malefic Nimbus, you can cause it to take necrotic damage equal to twice your spellcasting ability score (minimum 2) immediately after.

Violent Shadows

3rd-level Malefic feature
When a creature ends its turn in the darkness of your Malefic Nimbus, you can cause it to take 1d8 necrotic damage, or 2d8 necrotic damage if it is good-aligned.

Unholy Wings

4th-level Malefic feature
You can manifest spectral wings as a minor action. The wings are translucent and dark, but otherwise you choose their appearance when you gain this feature. While the wings are active, bright light in your space becomes dim light and you have a fly speed of 40 feet. The wings remain until you use another minor action to dismiss them or until you are reduced to 0 hit points.

Consuming Darkness

5th-level Malefic feature
When you manifest your Unholy Wings, you can empower them. If you do so, your Malefic Nimbus is also activated and remains active with the wings, no concentration required, and the distance of the darkness is doubled. However, you take 5 necrotic damage at the start of each of your turns as long as the wings are active. This damage can't be reduced or prevented.

Celestial Resistances

2nd-level Aasimar feature
You have resistance to radiant and necrotic damage.

Obscured

Obscured

When vision in an area is hindered by smoke, darkness, or a similar phenomenon, the area is obscured. There are two degrees of obscurity.

Lightly Obscured

In a lightly obscured area, such as dim light, patchy fog, or moderate foliage, creatures have disadvantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks that rely on sight.

Heavily Obscured

A heavily obscured area, such as darkness, opaque fog, or dense foliage, blocks vision entirely. Nothing in that area can be seen.

Light

Light

The presence or absence of light in an environment creates three categories of illumination: bright light, dim light, and darkness.

Bright Light

Bright light lets most creatures see normally. Even gloomy days provide bright light, as do torches, lanterns, fires, and other sources of illumination within a specific radius.

Dim Light

Dim light, also called shadows, creates a lightly obscured area. An area of dim light is usually a boundary between a source of bright light, such as a torch, and surrounding darkness. The soft light of twilight and dawn also counts as dim light. A particularly brilliant full moon might bathe the land in dim light.

Darkness

Darkness creates a heavily obscured area. Characters face darkness outdoors at night (even most moonlit nights), within the confines of an unlit dungeon or a subterranean vault, or in an area of magical darkness.

Concentration

Concentration

Some spells and other effects require you to maintain concentration in order to keep them active. If you lose concentration, such an effect ends. You can also choose to end concentration on any effect at any time (no action required).
If a spell must be maintained with concentration, that fact appears in its Duration entry, and the spell specifies how long you can concentrate on it.
Normal activity, such as moving and attacking, doesn't interfere with concentration. The following factors can break concentration:
  • You lose concentration if you invoke another effect that requires concentration. You can't concentrate on two effects at once.
  • Whenever you take damage while you are concentrating, you must make a Constitution saving throw to maintain your concentration. The DC equals 10 or half the damage you take, whichever number is higher. If you take damage from multiple sources, such as an arrow and a dragon's breath, you make a separate saving throw for each source of damage.
  • You lose concentration on an effect if you are incapacitated or if you die.
  • Certain environmental phenomena, such as a wave crashing over you while you're on a storm-tossed ship, may require you to succeed on a DC 10 Constitution saving throw to maintain concentration. This is at your DM's discretion.

Prone

Prone

While prone, you experience the following effects:
  • The only movement mode available to you is crawling.
  • You have disadvantage on attack rolls.
  • An attack roll against you has advantage if the attacker is within 5 feet of you. Otherwise, the attack roll has disadvantage.
You can end this condition by standing up, which requires spending half of your walk speed when you move.
This condition is commonly caused by falling, falling unconscious, and the trip attack.

Falling

Falling

When you begin falling, you immediately fall 60 feet. While falling, you drop another 180 feet at the start of each of your turns.
On impact, you take one size die of bludgeoning damage for every 10 feet you fell, to a maximum of 20 dice. You land prone, unless you avoid taking damage from the fall.
You can use your reaction to mitigate the damage from landing. When you do so, make an Acrobatics check and reduce the fall damage by half the result.
If you land in a creature's space, that creature must make a DC 10 Dexterity saving throw. It can choose to fail. On a failed save, the fall damage is split evenly between you and it.
