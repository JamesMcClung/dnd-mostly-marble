Religion

Most cultures in the world have their own religion. A people might worship one god, many gods, powerful beings, their own ancestors, or something else. Almost every religion has priests invested with genuine power, even if that power isn't necessarily divine in origin.

Gods

Gods have no direct influence over the world. They need worshippers to carry out most tasks on their behalf. In return for doing a god's bidding, a worshipper is given a bit of divine power to do with as they please, as long as it is not counter to their god's designs. Clerics, many paladins, and some druids rely exclusively on this power for spellcasting.

Since gods derive power from worship, they are in part beholden to their worshippers. A god may choose to reflect changes in his worshippers in order to prevent another god from taking his place, although doing so is taxing for the god. An extreme example of this is the Kuo-Toa goddess, Glishesh. The insane Kuo-Toa constantly revise their ideas of what Glishesh looks like and preaches, and she willingly adapts each time. In her struggle to hold on to her power, Glishesh has become as mad as her followers.

The above hints at an important point about the gods: they are transient. It is unclear how a god comes into being, but when sufficiently many people worship something new, a new god can appear to fill that niche. Similarly, if people stop worshipping a particular god, that god fades into history.

Spirits and Other Entities

Many people worship beings other than true gods. Dragons, demons, spirits, and many more creatures can grant power in exchange for service. This is the source of a warlock's powers. Some druids and paladins also draw their power from a powerful being or force.

Human Religions

Sonnentum

  • Religion of Sonnenland
    • Deeply ingrained in politics; head of the Church has great influence over the merchant lords
  • Hierarchical structure of clergymen
  • Has missionaries
  • Worships Die Sonne ("The Sun") aka Ihr Strahlkraft ("Her Radiance")
    • Stars are lesser angels, although only a few are important and have names
    • Planets are greater angels who serve Her Radiance directly
    • Der Mond ("The Moon"), head angel, watches over the land when She is away

Dogmas

  • Sunlight, moonlight, and starlight are holy and repel evil
  • Her Radiance sees all that happens under a clear daytime sky, and punishes wrongdoing
  • Evil is to be vanquished as the light vanquishes the dark
  • Observe the dawn each day to cleanse yourself of evils

Shamalism

  • Religion of Shamalian tribes
  • Gods teach lessons of different aspects of nature and manifest as animals
    • Gods do not grant powers or blessings; they only teach, and a follower is expected to benefit by heeding their lessons
  • All gods are revered except Majaeatan, who is feared
  • Practitioners use blood to power spells

Dogmas

  • Red is color of blood, which powers all (animal) life
  • Survival of the fittest: the strong and cunning have the right to rule, but must prove themselves

Pantheon

  • Majaeatan: the chaotic evil god of hunger
    • Can manifest as any animal, but always appears starved and with a bloody mouth
    • Influences people to commit acts of cannibalism and turn them into wendigos
  • Almawt: the chaotic neutral god of survival and death
    • Can manifest as any injured animal, especially a polar bear pierced by broken spears
    • Teaches that hardship may occur unfairly or randomly, but one must survive anyways
  • Alam: the neutral god of pain and wisdom
    • Manifests as a female bear, mammoth, or mountain lion
    • Teaches that pain is a means to wisdom, and true wisdom can only come from pain
  • Takathur: the lawful neutral god of victory, wealth, and sex
    • Manifests as a brightly-colored bird or a buck with large antlers
    • Teaches that a victor deserves that which they fought to obtain
  • Zumra: the lawful good god of war, cooperation, and order
    • Manifests as a wolf
    • Teaches that the group is stronger than the individual, provided the group has a clear hierarchy
  • Alsabr: the good god of patience and preparation
    • Manifests as a squirrel, bee, ant, or web-spinning spider
    • Teaches that challenges can be overcome with sufficient preparation and hard work

Female Clergy

  • Top: High Priestess: authority is second only to the gods themselves
    • Nomadic, traveling with her servants to each tribe over the course of a many-year cycle
  • Red Priestesses: lead their respective tribes in religious and political affairs
    • A Red Priestess becomes the High Priestess by killing her when she visits, taking her place
    • Capable of powerful blood magic
  • Priestesses: train under their Red Priestess, learning secrets of blood magic
    • Promoted to Red Priestess by High Priestess based on merit and when position is open
  • Acolytes: learn basics of the religion as they serve a Priestess
    • Red-haired girls are blessed by gods and pre-ordained to enter clergy
    • Eventually graduate to become a Priestess

Male Clergy

  • Top: Red Warlords: lead their respective tribes in hunting or battle
    • Title is given by Red Priestess based on merit, and can be revoked
  • Red Warriors: elite soldiers who can use blood magic to empower themselves
  • Warriors: competent soldiers versed in religious codes, training under Red Warriors in specialized blood magic
    • A Warrior becomes a Red Warrior when the Red Warlord recognizes their skill in battle and promotes them
  • Acolytes: learn basics of the religion as they serve a Warrior
    • Red-haired boys are blessed by gods and pre-ordained to enter clergy
    • Eventually graduate to become a Warrior

Agama Lama

  • Means "old religion"
  • Ancient, partially reconstructed religion of Tanatenga
    • Most modern Tengans practice the local religion, not this
  • Major gods had cities dedicated to them
    • Each city had a temple and a massive statue to a particular god or goddess
  • Gods take human forms and apparently made up a family
  • Believed to have had hundreds of gods, but only a few have been rediscovered and their names are unknown
    • The gods went too long without worship and most of them faded forever
    • Surviving gods do not remember anything, but retain than their personalities and beliefs
      • These gods are referred to by modern epithets, e.g. "Yang Mematikan" in Tengan <redacted>

      Remaining Pantheon

      • Yang Mematikan ("The Deathly One"): goddess of death and the underworld
        • Idealizes control over one's life
        • Reticent but stern
        • Commands the dead
      • Yang Tinggi ("The High One"): god of the sky, stars, and weather
        • Idealizes freedom
        • Brash and fickle
        • Commands storms
      • Yang Luas ("The Vast One"): goddess of the earth's surface and the harvest
        • Idealizes cultivation, preparation, and nurture
        • Patient and caring
        • Commands plants
      • Yang Perkasa ("The Mighty One"): god of champions and leadership
        • Idealizes bravery, strength, and authority
        • Commanding, bold, possibly reckless
        • Commands people
      • Yang Tersembunyi ("The Hidden One"): god of secrets
        • Idealizes knowledge, secrecy, and magical powers
        • Evasive and mysterious
        • Utilizes obscure and complex magic

      Rogue Gods

      As cultures come and go, gods usually come and go with them. Some gods survive the fall of their progenitors thanks to fragmented cults that worship them. These "rogue gods" are some of the oldest gods. They have to have a strong identity and simple ideals to reduce idealogical drift in their followers. New religions often seek to purge rogue gods and recruit their followers, but some rogue gods are respected even by missionaries.

      Rogue Gods in Ata

      • The Seeker: neutral god of hunting and the natural order of life
        • one of the oldest Atan gods, with both Human and Orc followers
        • symbol is an obsidian arrow
        • legend has it that an obsidian arrow appears in the quiver of a hunter who is about to succomb to his prey
        • respected (but not necessarily loved) by all major religions in Ata
      • The Mother: goddess of vengeance
        • formerly good goddess of kindness and neighborliness for a backwater pre-Etelaan region just south of the mountains
        • symbol is a broken heart
        • manifested the loving part of herself in a human heroine, The Daughter
          • heroine was a selfless healer, but was killed by orcs during an invasion
          • survivors of invasion swore vengeance on orcs, and The Mother became goddess of vengeance
        • followers abhor orcs and still want revenge
