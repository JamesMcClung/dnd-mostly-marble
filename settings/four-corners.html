Four Corners

The homepage to my "Four Corners" campaign setting. All of my notes are available here. These notes are mostly written from a human-centric point of view.

Overview

At the dawn of the world, the Primordials of Earth, Air, Water, and Fire were anathema to civilization. Each Primordial became known by a different name to each race; humans now call them Behemoth, Typhon, Leviathan, and Shaitan, respectively. They were responsible for all natural disasters, such as earthquakes, hurricanes, floods, and volcanic eruptions.

Of less tangible presence are the gods. There are hundreds of gods, with no clear hierarchy. Gods go in and out of style with each generation, since the strength of a god is dependent on the number and zeal of its adherents. As such, Humans have adopted thousands of gods over the millenia, and have discarded as many. Longer-lived races have held on to their gods for much longer, and the immortal Elves revere the same gods as they did in the beginning.

The entire world has been more-or-less fully explored for nearly a century. Shipping networks connect the economies of all major continents: the northeastern Gabroria, Verdania in the far east, the Pyrmies in the southwest, Ata in the west, and the Iceplains of the north.
